Photoshop: Most Powerful, Steepest Learning Curve

Latest AI features: Generative Fill, Generative Expand, object removal with Firefly.

What makes it stand out: The generative fill is legitimately magical. Describe what you want and it fills it in perfectly.

Cost: $55/month subscription

Learning curve: High. Not for casual users.

Canva: Easiest, Most Versatile

What I love: Magic editing features. Upload a photo, click "enhance" and AI improves it.

Unique feature: Magic Media generates images to add to your photos.

Strength: No learning curve. Anyone can use it.

Cost: Free tier or $13/month Pro

Luminar Neo: Best Value for Photo Enthusiasts

What it does: Professional photo editing with AI assistance. Removes objects, enhances sky, fixes lighting.

Strength: One-time payment ($99/year). No subscription.

Best for: Photographers, serious hobbyists.

Use Photoshop if: You need professional-grade results and know how to edit.
Use Canva if: You want simplicity and speed.
Use Luminar if: You're a photo enthusiast who hates subscriptions.

My Actual Workflow

Quick social media edits: Canva (30 seconds)

Professional client work: Photoshop (5-10 minutes)

Personal photos: Luminar Neo (2-3 minutes)

Verdict: For most people, Canva Pro ($13/month) covers 90% of needs. Photoshop if you're making a living from design.
