Best AI Tools for Dental Practices and Imaging in 2026

By Sarah Mitchell February 25, 2026 Updated: March 12, 2026 14 min read

Last September, I sat in a dental chair while my dentist pulled up my panoramic X-ray on a screen—and next to the standard image was a colorful AI overlay highlighting areas I never would have noticed. She pointed to a faint shadow near my lower right molar. "The AI flagged this as an early interproximal lesion," she explained. "I probably would have caught it in six months. The software caught it now."

That experience sent me down a rabbit hole of dental AI tools that lasted four months. I've since spoken with 15 dental professionals across solo practices, group clinics, and DSOs (Dental Service Organizations) to understand which AI tools actually deliver value—and which ones are expensive dashboards that collect dust after the initial excitement fades.

The State of Dental AI in 2026

The dental AI market crossed $1.28 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $3 billion by 2030. That explosive growth means there are now dozens of companies competing for your practice's subscription dollars. The challenge isn't finding AI tools—it's finding ones that integrate smoothly into existing workflows without disrupting your team's productivity.

Here's what I found after extensive testing and interviews.

1. Pearl — The Gold Standard for AI Radiograph Analysis

Pricing: Approximately $250–$500/month per location. Setup fee typically around $1,500 (Patterson Dental currently offers 50% off setup, saving up to $750 through April 2026).

FDA Status: FDA-cleared for both 2D X-rays and 3D CBCT imaging—the first and only dental AI with both clearances.

Pearl's "Second Opinion" product is the most widely adopted dental AI in the world, used by over 50,000 clinicians. I watched it in action at three different practices, and the consistency was remarkable. The system analyzes every radiograph in real time, applying color-coded overlays that highlight potential caries, periapical pathologies, calculus, bone loss, and 100+ other conditions.

Dr. James Chen, who runs a three-operatory practice in Portland, told me his case acceptance rate jumped from 62% to 81% within four months of implementing Pearl. "When patients can see the AI highlighting the same thing I'm explaining verbally, the trust factor goes through the roof," he said. "It's like having an objective second opinion that validates my treatment recommendations."

Pearl's newer "Practice Intelligence" module goes beyond imaging—it analyzes your practice data to identify trends in treatment patterns, production per provider, and missed diagnosis rates. One DSO managing 40 locations told me this module helped them identify a 23% variation in caries detection rates across their providers, leading to targeted training that standardized care quality.

What I didn't love: The integration process with some legacy practice management systems can be rocky. One office I visited spent three weeks troubleshooting connectivity issues with their older Eaglesoft installation.

2. Overjet — Best for Insurance Verification and Multi-Location Practices

Pricing: $250–$500/month per location. Custom pricing for DSOs with 10+ locations.

FDA Status: FDA-cleared for dental AI analysis.

Overjet differentiates itself with its insurance-focused features. The platform doesn't just analyze radiographs—it maps findings directly to insurance codes and generates documentation that supports claims. For practices that deal with frequent insurance denials, this is a game-changer.

The color-coded radiographic overlays are visually striking and highly effective for patient communication. During my observation at a busy group practice in Dallas, I watched the hygienist use Overjet's overlays during a patient consult. The patient, who had been declining recommended treatment for months, agreed to proceed after seeing the AI-highlighted bone loss progression compared to their previous visit.

Overjet's administrative AI also handles prior authorization workflows, automatically attaching clinical documentation to claims. One office manager told me it cut her denial rate from 18% to 6% in the first quarter of use.

Drawback: The platform is heavily optimized for US insurance workflows. International practices or cash-pay focused offices won't get as much value from the insurance features.

3. Denti.AI — Best All-in-One Platform for Small Practices

Pricing: Custom pricing; generally more affordable than Pearl/Overjet. Contact for quotes.

Denti.AI packs three powerful tools into one platform: an AI dental scribe, voice-activated perio charting, and X-ray analysis. The integration of these three functions is what makes it special—rather than subscribing to separate tools for documentation, charting, and imaging analysis, you get everything in one workflow.

The AI scribe listens to your clinical narration and converts it into structured SOAP notes automatically. I watched Dr. Lisa Patel use it during five consecutive patient exams, and the documentation quality was impressive—accurate clinical terminology, proper formatting, and completed notes within seconds of the patient leaving the chair. She estimated it saves her 45 minutes per day compared to manual charting.

The voice perio charting feature lets hygienists call out probe depths verbally while both hands remain in the patient's mouth. It achieved 96% adoption after just three training sessions, according to Denti.AI's data—a remarkably low learning curve for clinical software.

Limitation: The imaging analysis capabilities, while solid, aren't as comprehensive as Pearl's or Overjet's dedicated platforms. If radiograph analysis is your primary need, consider the specialized tools.

4. Diagnocat — Best for CBCT and Advanced Imaging

Pricing: Per-scan pricing model available. Monthly subscription plans for high-volume practices. Contact for specific pricing.

Diagnocat specializes in automated diagnoses and structured reports from CBCTs, panoramic images, and intraoral radiographs. If your practice does significant implant planning or endodontic work requiring 3D imaging, Diagnocat's analysis capabilities are among the most advanced I've seen.

The platform generates comprehensive diagnostic reports from CBCT scans in minutes—a process that traditionally requires 20-30 minutes of manual analysis per scan. An oral surgeon I spoke with said Diagnocat catches findings in 3D volumes that even experienced radiologists occasionally miss, particularly small periapical pathologies in posterior regions obscured by overlapping anatomy.

5. Dental Monitoring — Best for Orthodontic Practices

Pricing: Per-patient pricing model. Volume discounts for high-case-load practices. Contact for quotes.

If your practice focuses on orthodontics or clear aligner therapy, Dental Monitoring is essentially a remote monitoring system powered by AI. Patients take smartphone photos of their teeth, and the AI analyzes over 130 oral events—tooth movement, gingival changes, attachment loss, and more—between office visits.

For aligner practices, this is transformative. One orthodontist in Miami replaced 40% of her routine progress check appointments with remote AI monitoring, freeing chair time for new consultations and complex procedures. Patient satisfaction actually increased because they didn't have to drive to the office for a five-minute check.

6. ScanO — Best Budget Option for Patient Communication

Pricing: Affordable subscription plans designed for solo and small practices. Contact for pricing.

ScanO focuses on the patient communication angle of dental AI. The platform creates visual, easy-to-understand reports from dental scans that patients can access on their phones. It's not trying to compete with Pearl or Overjet on diagnostic depth—instead, it excels at turning clinical findings into patient-friendly visuals that drive case acceptance.

7. Planmeca Romexis with AI Modules — Best Hardware-Integrated Solution

Pricing: Bundled with Planmeca imaging hardware. Software licensing varies by module. Contact Planmeca for quotes.

If you're already using Planmeca imaging equipment, their Romexis software now includes AI-powered modules that integrate directly with the hardware. The advantage here is seamless workflow—no third-party integrations, no API connections to troubleshoot. The AI analysis happens natively within the same software you use to capture images.

Making the Right Choice for Your Practice

After months of research, here's my practical advice for different practice types:

  • Solo general practices: Start with Denti.AI for the all-in-one value proposition. The combined scribe, charting, and imaging analysis covers your core needs without multiple subscriptions.
  • Group practices (3-10 providers): Pearl's Second Opinion delivers the most consistent ROI through improved case acceptance and standardized diagnostic quality across providers.
  • DSOs and large groups: Overjet's insurance verification AI and multi-location analytics make it the strongest choice for scale.
  • Orthodontic practices: Dental Monitoring is nearly essential if you're running a high-volume aligner practice.
  • Practices doing heavy implant/endo work: Add Diagnocat for CBCT analysis—the 3D diagnostic reports alone justify the cost.

The dental AI space is maturing rapidly. Two years ago, these tools were novelties. Today, practices without some form of AI diagnostic support are increasingly at a competitive disadvantage—both in clinical quality and patient experience. The investment range of $250-$500/month is substantial, but every practice owner I interviewed who'd been using these tools for six months or more said the ROI was clear and measurable.

My strongest advice: request demos from your top two choices and run them simultaneously in your practice for a month. The tool that your team actually uses consistently is the right one, regardless of feature comparisons on paper.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does AI dental imaging software cost?

Most AI dental imaging platforms cost between $250 and $500 per month per practice location. Pearl and Overjet typically quote in this range. Some platforms like Denti.AI offer different pricing models that can be more economical for smaller practices.

Is AI dental imaging FDA cleared?

Pearl was the first dental AI to receive FDA clearance for both 2D X-ray and 3D CBCT image analysis. Overjet and VideaHealth also have FDA clearance. Always verify FDA status before purchasing, as non-cleared tools cannot be used for clinical diagnoses in the US.

Can AI replace a dentist for diagnosis?

No. AI dental imaging tools are decision-support systems that work alongside dentists as a "second opinion." They can flag potential findings the human eye might miss, but the final diagnosis and treatment planning always remains with the licensed dentist.

What is the ROI of dental AI imaging software?

Practices using AI imaging tools report detecting 37% more pathologies on average, with case acceptance rates increasing 20-35%. At $250-500/month, most practices recoup the investment within 2-3 months through additional identified treatment and improved patient acceptance.

Which dental AI imaging tool is best for solo practices?

Denti.AI offers the best all-in-one value with combined AI scribe, voice perio charting, and X-ray analysis. Pearl's Second Opinion is excellent for high radiograph volumes. ScanO is another affordable option focused on patient-facing visual reports.
