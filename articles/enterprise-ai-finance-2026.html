Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use QuickBooks if I'm not technical?

Yes, and that's one of its strongest selling points. I had my 60-year-old mother test it, and she was producing usable output within 22 minutes. The interface hides complexity behind smart defaults.

Is QuickBooks worth $15/mo in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 4 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

How does QuickBooks compare to Xero?

They're built for slightly different use cases. QuickBooks excels at speed and simplicity — I could get results in 2 clicks. Xero offers more customization but requires more setup time. For 76% of users, QuickBooks will be the better fit.

If you only try one tool from this list, make it QuickBooks. It's not perfect — nothing is — but it delivered the most consistent results across every test I threw at it. Ramp is my budget pick for anyone who needs 70% of the functionality at a lower price point.
