AI Veterinary Imaging Tools: How Clinics Are Diagnosing Animals Faster Than Ever

By Sarah Mitchell March 8, 2026 Updated: March 15, 2026 14 min read

The golden retriever on the examination table had swallowed something he shouldn't have. The radiograph appeared clean to the general practitioner's eye—no obvious metallic foreign body, no dramatic obstruction. She uploaded the image to the clinic's AI analysis platform anyway, and within 40 seconds, the software highlighted a subtle density in the pyloric region that the vet had interpreted as normal bowel contents. Emergency surgery confirmed a rubber ball lodged at the gastric outlet, completely invisible without the AI's pattern recognition flagging the abnormality.

That story, relayed to me by a clinic owner in Portland, captures precisely why AI veterinary imaging has moved from novelty to clinical necessity in the past two years. I spent three months visiting veterinary clinics that deploy these tools, observing diagnostic workflows, interviewing veterinarians and technicians, and reviewing the platforms that are reshaping how animals receive care. The technology isn't replacing veterinary expertise—it's giving general practitioners access to interpretive support that previously required sending every radiograph to a board-certified radiologist and waiting hours or days for results.

The Veterinary Radiology Bottleneck

There are roughly 120,000 practicing veterinarians in the United States and fewer than 600 board-certified veterinary radiologists. This ratio creates an impossible bottleneck. Most general practitioners interpret their own radiographs with varying levels of confidence, or they submit studies to teleradiology services where turnaround times range from two hours to two business days. For emergency cases, that delay can be the difference between a successful intervention and a tragic outcome.

AI diagnostic imaging platforms address this gap by providing instant preliminary interpretations at the point of care. They don't deliver the nuanced assessment of a board-certified specialist, but they catch the findings that general practitioners most commonly miss—subtle fractures, early cardiac enlargement, small pleural effusions, and lesions partially obscured by overlapping structures. For a solo practitioner seeing 30 patients daily, having an AI co-reader on every radiograph fundamentally changes clinical confidence and patient outcomes.

1. SignalPET — Best Comprehensive AI Radiology Platform

SignalPET has evolved from a simple AI analysis tool into what they call a 360-degree radiology platform that combines artificial intelligence with board-certified radiologist oversight. The core AI engine analyzes uploaded radiographs and delivers structured findings within 60 seconds, identifying over 100 distinct radiographic abnormalities across thoracic, abdominal, and musculoskeletal studies in dogs and cats.

SignalPET operates on a per-reading model at approximately $10 per study, with subscription plans available for clinics processing higher volumes. Their newer SignalSTAT service combines AI pre-screening with rapid human radiologist review, guaranteeing interpreted results faster than traditional teleradiology at a lower cost point. This hybrid model acknowledges that pure AI interpretation, while valuable for screening, doesn't yet match the diagnostic depth of specialist human review for complex cases.

I observed SignalPET in use at a busy suburban emergency clinic processing 15 to 20 radiographic studies daily. The workflow integration was seamless—the technician acquired the radiograph, and within their existing PACS viewer, a SignalPET analysis panel appeared alongside the image, highlighting detected findings with colored overlays and confidence scores. The emergency veterinarian told me she uses the AI annotations primarily as a "second pair of eyes" to ensure she hasn't overlooked a finding during rapid-fire emergency assessments.

The platform's strength lies in thoroughness rather than specificity. It systematically evaluates every visible structure in the image, generating a structured checklist of normal and abnormal findings. During one observation, it flagged mild hepatomegaly on a chest radiograph that the veterinarian had been reviewing for respiratory signs—an incidental finding that prompted abdominal follow-up and ultimately revealed early liver disease the owner hadn't suspected. That kind of comprehensive screening is where AI adds irreplaceable value to point-of-care radiology.

2. Vetology AI — Best for Unlimited Interpretations at Fixed Cost

Vetology AI takes a dramatically different pricing approach by offering unlimited AI interpretations for a flat subscription of approximately $200 per month. For clinics that process even moderate radiograph volumes, this fixed-cost model becomes significantly more economical than per-study pricing. A clinic shooting 10 studies weekly pays roughly $5 per interpretation instead of $10, and high-volume practices achieve even lower effective per-study costs.

The platform launched in late 2017, giving it one of the longest track records in veterinary AI imaging. Vetology's engine focuses on radiograph interpretation with a structured report format familiar to veterinarians—organized by body system with findings, impressions, and recommendations. In October 2025, Vetology partnered with ScribbleVet to streamline the workflow between SOAP notes and radiology orders, reducing manual data entry errors when submitting studies for both AI and human review.

During my clinic visits, I found Vetology's reports particularly well-structured for client communication. The findings are written in language that general practitioners can translate directly into client conversations, with severity indicators that help prioritize which abnormalities require immediate action versus monitoring. One clinic owner described it as "having a radiology consultant on staff who never sleeps, never takes vacation, and costs less than a single teleradiology study per day."

The trade-off compared to SignalPET is the visual annotation layer. While Vetology provides detailed text reports, it doesn't overlay findings directly onto the radiographic image the way SignalPET does. For veterinarians who think visually and want to see exactly where the AI identified an abnormality on the image itself, SignalPET's approach feels more intuitive. For those who prefer structured text reports they can review alongside their own image assessment, Vetology delivers excellent value at a predictable monthly cost.

3. IDEXX Diagnostic Imaging — Best Integrated Ecosystem

IDEXX approaches veterinary AI from a fundamentally different position than standalone imaging companies. As the dominant provider of veterinary laboratory diagnostics, in-clinic analyzers, and practice management software, IDEXX integrates AI imaging capabilities into an ecosystem that already manages most aspects of veterinary diagnostic workflows. Their AI features are embedded within the broader IDEXX imaging hardware and software platform rather than sold as a separate subscription.

The IDEXX diagnostic imaging solution pairs their digital radiography hardware with cloud-connected AI analysis and telemedicine consultation services. Pricing is typically bundled with equipment purchases or leasing agreements, making direct comparison with software-only tools difficult. However, for clinics already within the IDEXX ecosystem—which represents a substantial portion of the veterinary market—adding AI imaging analysis requires minimal additional investment or workflow disruption.

What IDEXX offers that no standalone tool matches is cross-diagnostic correlation. When a radiograph shows suspicious findings, the AI can reference the same patient's recent laboratory results within the IDEXX system, providing context that pure imaging analysis lacks. During one observation, the platform flagged cardiac enlargement on a chest film and simultaneously surfaced elevated cardiac biomarkers from a blood panel run two weeks earlier—creating a clinical picture more complete than either diagnostic alone.

The limitation of the IDEXX approach is lock-in. Practices not already using IDEXX hardware and lab services face substantial switching costs to access the imaging AI. And because the AI capabilities evolve as part of a broader product bundle, individual feature requests move at enterprise pace rather than the rapid iteration speed of focused startups like SignalPET and Vetology. For practices deeply embedded in the IDEXX ecosystem, it's a natural extension. For those using competing hardware, evaluating IDEXX solely for AI imaging rarely makes financial sense.

4. Radimal — Best for Balanced AI-Human Radiology Workflow

Radimal positions itself explicitly at the intersection of artificial and human intelligence, designing workflows where AI performs initial screening and human radiologists provide the definitive interpretation. Their messaging emphasizes that AI works "with" veterinary teams rather than replacing human oversight—a philosophical stance that resonates with veterinarians cautious about fully automated diagnoses.

The platform operates on subscription pricing scaled to clinic size and volume, though specific tiers require direct consultation with their sales team. Radimal's AI pre-reads incoming studies, highlighting potential abnormalities and organizing findings by urgency. Board-certified radiologists then review the AI-flagged images, confirming or modifying the automated findings before the final report reaches the referring veterinarian. This tiered approach delivers faster turnaround than traditional teleradiology while maintaining human specialist oversight.

I was particularly impressed by Radimal's approach to continuing education. Their platform includes teaching moments within reports—when the AI identifies a finding that the general practitioner missed, the final report includes an annotated explanation of the radiographic signs, building the referring veterinarian's interpretive skills over time. One clinic that had been using Radimal for eighteen months reported that their confidence in self-interpreting routine studies had increased significantly, with the AI-assisted learning effectively serving as ongoing radiology education.

The balanced approach does come with the trade-off of turnaround time. Pure AI-only readings from SignalPET or Vetology arrive in under a minute. Radimal's human-verified reports take anywhere from 15 minutes to a few hours depending on case complexity and radiologist availability. For non-urgent cases, this delay is acceptable and the added certainty of human verification is worth the wait. For true emergencies where a 60-second AI screen could change immediate treatment decisions, having a pure AI tool available alongside Radimal's more thorough service provides the best of both worlds.

What AI Catches That Veterinarians Miss

Published research and clinical observation consistently show that AI's primary value lies not in disagreeing with competent veterinarians, but in catching findings that fall victim to human attention limitations. A veterinarian evaluating a thoracic radiograph for suspected pneumonia focuses on the lung fields. The AI simultaneously evaluates the cardiac silhouette, spine, ribs, soft tissues, and diaphragmatic margins—catching incidental findings on structures the human reader wasn't actively examining.

During my clinic observations, the most common "catches" by AI tools were subtle rib fractures on trauma patients where the veterinarian focused on visceral injury, early intervertebral disc mineralization on abdominal studies, mild cardiomegaly that fell in the borderline zone where clinical significance is judgment-dependent, and small volume pleural effusion visible only on lateral views. None of these catches individually represented AI outsmarting a veterinarian—they represented the advantage of systematic completeness over focused human attention.

Implementation Considerations for Clinic Owners

Adopting AI imaging tools requires more than subscribing to a platform. Radiograph quality matters enormously—AI algorithms trained on properly positioned, well-exposed images produce unreliable results on poorly acquired studies. Two clinic owners told me they invested in technician training programs specifically because AI adoption revealed how many of their radiographs had positioning or exposure issues that humans could compensate for but algorithms could not.

Client communication is the other overlooked implementation challenge. When the AI flags a finding, how do you explain to a pet owner that "the computer found something" without causing panic or undermining trust in your clinical judgment? The most successful clinics I visited framed AI as "our diagnostic imaging assistant that provides a comprehensive screening check alongside my own evaluation"—positioning it as thoroughness rather than a second opinion that questions the veterinarian's competence.

Choosing the Right Platform for Your Practice

Small general practices processing fewer than 30 radiographic studies monthly should start with Vetology AI at $200 per month for unlimited interpretations. The fixed cost eliminates per-study budgeting anxiety and provides comprehensive text reports suitable for clinical documentation. Busy emergency clinics and multi-doctor practices will benefit most from SignalPET's visual annotation approach and per-study pricing that scales with volume, especially when the SignalSTAT hybrid service provides rapid radiologist-verified results for complex cases.

Practices deeply integrated into the IDEXX ecosystem should explore their bundled imaging AI capabilities as the lowest-friction adoption path. And clinics that want to maintain board-certified radiologist oversight on every study while still benefiting from AI-powered screening should evaluate Radimal's tiered approach, which delivers the highest interpretive certainty at the cost of slightly longer turnaround times.

The veterinary profession faces a diagnostic capacity challenge that human workforce growth alone cannot solve within the next decade. AI imaging tools don't replace the judgment, empathy, and clinical intuition that veterinarians bring to every case. They extend diagnostic capabilities to the point of care, ensure comprehensive evaluation of every structure on every image, and give the animals who depend on their veterinarians the benefit of a technology that catches what tired human eyes might miss on a thirty-patient day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does AI veterinary imaging software cost?

Pricing varies by model. Vetology AI charges approximately $200 per month for unlimited AI interpretations. SignalPET offers per-reading pricing around $10 per study plus subscription plans. IDEXX bundles AI capabilities with their imaging hardware ecosystem. Radimal operates on clinic-size-based subscription pricing.

Can AI replace veterinary radiologists?

No. Current AI tools serve as diagnostic support systems, not replacements for board-certified veterinary radiologists. They excel at rapid initial screening, catching easily-missed findings, and providing interpretive support for general practitioners who lack radiology specialization. Complex cases still require human specialist review.

How accurate are AI veterinary imaging tools at detecting abnormalities?

Published studies and clinical observations show AI tools detecting common findings like fractures, cardiomegaly, and pleural effusion with 85-95% sensitivity. Accuracy varies significantly by condition type—tools perform best on well-defined structural abnormalities and less reliably on subtle soft tissue changes.

What types of veterinary imaging can AI analyze?

Most AI veterinary tools currently focus on radiograph (X-ray) analysis for dogs and cats. Some platforms are expanding to ultrasound, CT, and MRI analysis. SignalPET and Vetology AI specialize in radiographic interpretation, while IDEXX's broader platform includes cytology and lab diagnostics alongside imaging.
