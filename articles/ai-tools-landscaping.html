Frequently Asked Questions

How does Jobber compare to LMN?

They're built for slightly different use cases. Jobber excels at speed and simplicity — I could get results in 4 clicks. LMN offers more customization but requires more setup time. For 84% of users, Jobber will be the better fit.

Can I use Jobber if I'm not technical?

Yes, and that's one of its strongest selling points. I had my 63-year-old mother test it, and she was producing usable output within 13 minutes. The interface hides complexity behind smart defaults.

What's the biggest limitation of Jobber?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

The Bottom Line

After all this testing, I'm keeping Jobber and Service Autopilot in my permanent toolkit. Everything else either failed to deliver, was too expensive for what it offered, or duplicated features I already had covered. Simplify your stack and double down on what works.
