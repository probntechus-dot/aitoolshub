Frequently Asked Questions

What's the biggest limitation of Duolingo?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 3x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

Will these tools replace human professionals?

After testing 22 tools in this space, my answer is a firm no — but they'll replace professionals who refuse to use them. The people getting the best results treat AI as a force multiplier, handling the 73% of work that's repetitive so humans can focus on strategy and creativity.

Is Duolingo worth $14/mo in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 3 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

The Bottom Line

Here's what I tell friends who ask: start with Notion if you're just exploring. If you're serious about results, go straight to Duolingo and skip the 'trying everything' phase I went through. Your time is worth more than the price difference.
