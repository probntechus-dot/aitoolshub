Two Categories of AI in Optometry

AI in eye care falls into two distinct categories, and understanding the difference is crucial for making smart purchasing decisions:

Practice Management AI: Software that handles scheduling, documentation, billing, patient communication, and business analytics. Think RevolutionEHR, MaximEyes, and Eyefinity.

Diagnostic AI: Systems that analyze retinal images, OCT scans, and visual field data to assist with clinical diagnoses. Think LumineticsCore, EyeArt, and AEYE Health.

Most practices need both, but they serve completely different functions. Let me break down the best options in each category.

RevolutionEHR is the platform I encountered most frequently during my research—and for good reason. 2. MaximEyes — Most Comprehensive Feature Set

MaximEyes is the Swiss Army knife of optometry software. It bundles EHR, practice management, image management, and medical billing into a single comprehensive platform. The image management module is particularly strong—it integrates with most major ophthalmic imaging devices and displays images alongside clinical notes in a unified interface.

The AI features in MaximEyes focus on workflow optimization. The system learns your exam patterns over time and pre-populates exam fields based on the appointment type and patient history. For a routine comprehensive exam, this can shave 3-5 minutes off documentation time per patient.

The analytics dashboard uses AI to identify practice performance trends—tracking metrics like capture rate (percentage of patients who buy glasses/contacts after their exam), recall compliance, and revenue per patient visit. One practice owner told me the analytics revealed that her associate OD's capture rate was 15 percentage points lower than hers, prompting targeted coaching that improved the associate's numbers within two months.

Drawback: The $350/month price point is significant for solo practitioners, and the comprehensive feature set means a longer learning curve. Budget for at least two weeks of dedicated onboarding time.

Diagnostic AI: Retinal Screening and Clinical Decision Support

6. LumineticsCore (formerly IDx-DR) — The Pioneer in Autonomous AI Screening

Pricing: Device licensing plus per-scan fees. Contact Digital Diagnostics for specific pricing. Reimbursable under CPT code 92229 at approximately $40-$60 per screening.

LumineticsCore made history in 2018 as the first FDA-authorized autonomous AI diagnostic system in any field of medicine. It screens for diabetic retinopathy at the point of care without requiring a specialist to interpret the results—the AI makes the diagnosis autonomously.

For optometry practices, this is a practice-builder. Every diabetic patient who walks through your door is a candidate for AI retinal screening, and it's billable under CPT 92229. Dr. Jennifer Hwang, who added LumineticsCore to her practice 18 months ago, screens an average of 12 diabetic patients per week. "At roughly $45 per screening reimbursement, that's over $2,000/month in revenue from a service that takes my tech less than 5 minutes per patient," she told me.

The clinical value is equally significant. LumineticsCore detects more-than-mild diabetic retinopathy and diabetic macular edema with sensitivity exceeding 87% and specificity above 90%. Patients get immediate results instead of waiting weeks for a specialist referral, and early detection directly preserves vision.

7. EyeArt by Eyenuk — Best for High-Volume Screening Programs

Pricing: Volume-based pricing. Contact Eyenuk for quotes. Also reimbursable under CPT 92229.

EyeArt is designed for scalability. While LumineticsCore requires specific hardware, EyeArt works with a broader range of fundus cameras, making it more flexible for practices with existing imaging equipment. The cloud-based AI analysis processes images rapidly and returns results in under a minute.

EyeArt's validation studies are extensive—tested on over 900,000 images with sensitivity and specificity comparable to LumineticsCore. For practices considering adding AI retinal screening but reluctant to invest in new hardware, EyeArt's compatibility with existing equipment lowers the barrier to entry significantly.

Emerging AI Applications Worth Watching

Beyond established tools, several AI applications are gaining traction in optometry:

  • AI-powered visual field analysis: Tools that identify glaucoma progression patterns earlier than traditional statistical methods, allowing intervention before measurable vision loss.
  • Anterior segment AI: Systems analyzing slit-lamp images for dry eye grading, cataract staging, and contact lens fitting optimization.
  • Myopia management AI: Predictive tools that model axial length progression in pediatric patients, helping determine when to initiate myopia control interventions.
  • AI-assisted refraction: Systems like Smart Vision Labs that use wavefront technology and AI to generate objective refractions, potentially streamlining the refraction workflow.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI-powered optometry software in 2026?

RevolutionEHR ($199-$299/month) leads for cloud-based practice management with AI workflow optimization. MaximEyes ($350/month) offers the most comprehensive feature set. Barti is the newest AI-native contender with the most modern interface.

Is AI retinal screening FDA approved for optometry practices?

Yes. Four FDA-cleared autonomous AI retinal screening systems exist: LumineticsCore (formerly IDx-DR), EyeArt by Eyenuk, AEYE Diagnostic Screening, and Retina-AI Galaxy. These can autonomously screen for diabetic retinopathy at the point of care.

How much does optometry practice management software cost?

Pricing ranges from free (WINK) to $350+/month (MaximEyes). RevolutionEHR starts at approximately $199/month. Eyefinity uses custom pricing. Most platforms require annual contracts and may charge setup fees.

Can optometrists bill for AI retinal screening?

Yes. AI-assisted diabetic retinal screening is reimbursable under CPT code 92229. Medicare and most private insurers cover it for diabetic patients, with reimbursement typically ranging from $40-$60 per screening.

Should I switch from my current optometry software to an AI-powered platform?

Consider switching if your current platform lacks cloud access, mobile functionality, or automated patient communications, or if documentation consumes more than 30 minutes daily. Budget 2-4 weeks for the transition period and verify data migration support.
