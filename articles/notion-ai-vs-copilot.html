Notion AI vs Microsoft Copilot: Which Should You Use?

Both Notion AI and Microsoft Copilot promise to automate your work. But they solve different problems. Here's my comparison from 60 days of testing.

Notion AI: Best for Knowledge Workers

What it does: Generates content, writes summaries, translates text, brainstorms ideas—all within Notion. Database query is its killer feature.

Best for: Teams already using Notion. Excellent for generating blog posts, summaries, and creative writing.

Advertisement

Limitation: Works only within Notion. No Word integration, no email assistance.

Microsoft Copilot: Best for Office Users

What it does: Integrates with Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint. Write emails with AI. Analyze spreadsheets. Create presentations.

Best for: Microsoft 365 subscribers. Especially powerful in Excel for data analysis and in Word for document generation.

Limitation: Locked into Microsoft ecosystem. No standalone value outside Office apps.

Bottom Line: Use Notion AI if you work in Notion. Use Copilot if you live in Microsoft Office. Don't use both.

My Verdict

For individuals: Notion AI ($10/month) is cheaper and more focused.

For organizations: Copilot ($20/month) because 90% of enterprises use Microsoft Office.

My personal choice? Neither exclusively. I use Notion AI for content work and ChatGPT for everything else. Most people won't need both Notion AI and Copilot.

What to Look for When Choosing

Before committing to any tool, ask yourself these questions:

  • What's your actual use case? Don't pay for features you'll never use.
  • What's your budget? Factor in not just monthly cost, but time invested in learning.
  • How does it integrate? A tool that doesn't play nice with your existing stack creates more problems than it solves.
  • What's the switching cost? Some tools make it easy to export your data. Others hold it hostage.
  • How's the support? When things break at 2 AM, you want responsive help.
