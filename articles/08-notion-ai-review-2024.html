I need to be upfront: I love Notion. Have for years. So when they launched Notion AI, I was both excited and nervous ‚Äî excited because AI in my second brain sounds amazing, nervous because I've seen many beloved apps ruin themselves by bolting on AI features that don't fit.

Eight months later, I can give you an honest verdict. The good news: Notion AI is genuinely useful. The honest news: it's only genuinely useful if you're already using Notion the right way.

Worth it if: You use Notion daily and have significant notes/docs to work with.

What Notion AI Actually Does

When people say "Notion AI," they mean two different things that are worth separating:

AI writing assistance ‚Äî Available on any page, lets you generate content, edit writing, change tone, translate, and summarize. Think of it as ChatGPT built into Notion.

AI across your workspace ‚Äî The more powerful (and newer) feature. You can ask questions about your entire Notion workspace and get answers pulled from across all your documents.

Most reviews focus on the writing assistance. I want to talk about the second feature, because that's where Notion AI becomes genuinely different from anything else on the market.

The Feature That Changed How I Work: AI Q&A Across Workspace

Here's a real example from last month. I had a client ask me about a specific piece of advice I'd given them in a meeting 4 months ago. I couldn't remember the details. With old Notion, I'd search, scroll through meeting notes, maybe find it in 15 minutes.

With Notion AI, I typed: "What did I recommend to [Client Name] about their pricing strategy in Q3?" Notion AI searched my 400+ pages of meeting notes, client docs, and strategy documents, and gave me a specific answer with a link to the exact page.

Took 8 seconds. I found the exact recommendation and context.

This is the feature that makes Notion AI irreplaceable for me. I have 3 years of notes, meeting summaries, project docs, and client info in Notion. Being able to query all of that with natural language is genuinely life-changing for knowledge workers.

Writing Assistance: Solid, Not Amazing

The writing features are good but not standout compared to dedicated writing AI tools.

I use them most often for:

  • Turning bullet-point notes into proper paragraphs
  • Summarizing long meeting transcripts
  • Drafting first-pass action items from meeting notes
  • Improving the clarity of SOPs and documentation

What I don't use Notion AI for: creative writing, marketing copy, or anything where I'd want the full power of GPT-4. For that, I still use ChatGPT directly and paste into Notion.

The writing quality is roughly GPT-3.5 level ‚Äî capable but not exceptional. For editing and summarizing, that's plenty. For generating content from scratch, you'll notice the ceiling.

The 5 Notion AI Use Cases I Actually Use Daily

1. Meeting Note Processing

After every call, I paste the raw transcript or voice memo transcription into Notion and ask AI to: create a clean summary, extract action items with owners, and flag any decisions made. What used to take 20-30 minutes takes 5 minutes. This alone justifies the $10/month.

2. Project Status Summaries

I manage 8-10 active client projects. Each has its own Notion page with dozens of sub-pages. Every Friday, I ask AI to "summarize the current status and open items for [Project Name]." It scans all connected pages and gives me a 5-bullet status update.

3. SOP Writing and Improvement

When I create a new process, I write rough bullet points and ask AI to turn it into a proper standard operating procedure with numbered steps. The result still needs editing, but starting from a structured draft is 3x faster than writing from scratch.

4. Content Research Recall

I save articles, research, and notes to Notion constantly. When working on a new client project, I ask "What have I saved about [topic]?" and get a synthesized answer from months of research. Like having a research assistant who's read everything I've ever read.

5. Template Generation

Need a project brief template? Client onboarding checklist? Content calendar structure? Notion AI generates first drafts I then customize. Not groundbreaking, but saves the "starting from blank page" anxiety.

Where Notion AI Disappoints

Honest section incoming:

It can be slow. The workspace Q&A feature sometimes takes 15-20 seconds to respond, especially if your workspace is large. For quick lookups, this kills the momentum.

Writing quality has a ceiling. For anything creative or nuanced, you'll feel the limitation. It's good at structured tasks, less good at anything requiring real creativity or technical depth.

Context window limits. For very long documents (50+ pages), AI sometimes misses details in the early sections. I've had it give me incomplete summaries that missed key early points.

Price stacks up in teams. The $10/month is per user. For a 10-person team, that's $100/month added to your Notion bill. At that scale, you'll want to be sure everyone uses it regularly.
