Semrush vs Ahrefs in 2026: I Tested Both. Here's What I Found

⏱ 10 min read
By Sarah Mitchell March 14, 2026
📝 Disclosure: Some links in this article are affiliate links. If you purchase through them, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. We only recommend tools we've personally tested.

Every year someone writes a "Semrush vs Ahrefs" article, and every year it reads like a press release for whoever's paying the affiliate commission. I wanted to do something different.

I ran both tools side by side for three months on three websites — a niche blog pulling 40K monthly sessions, a SaaS company site, and a local business with five locations. Same queries, same sites, same time period. I took notes on everything.

Here's what I actually found. No sugar-coating.

Keyword Research: Semrush Goes Wider, Ahrefs Goes Deeper

I started with a simple test. I plugged the seed keyword "email marketing software" into both tools and compared what came back.

Semrush returned over 45,000 keyword ideas, broken into categories like related keywords, questions, and phrase matches. Each keyword got an intent label — informational, navigational, commercial, or transactional. That intent data is incredibly useful when you're building a content strategy because it tells you what people want when they search, not just how often they search.

Ahrefs returned about 38,000 keyword ideas for the same seed term. Fewer keywords, but the data felt more precise. Ahrefs shows clicks data — not just search volume, but how many people actually click a result after searching. That distinction matters more than you'd think. A keyword with 10,000 monthly searches but only 4,000 clicks means 60% of searchers get their answer from the SERP without clicking anything.

For building content calendars and topic clusters, I preferred Semrush's intent data. For evaluating whether a keyword is actually worth targeting, Ahrefs' click data gave me better signals.

Backlink Analysis: Ahrefs Is Still the King

This has been true for years, and it's still true in 2026. Ahrefs has the largest and most frequently updated backlink index in the industry. Period.

I compared backlink profiles for my SaaS site on both platforms. Ahrefs found 2,847 referring domains. Semrush found 2,412. That's a 15% gap — and those missing domains included some genuinely useful link building opportunities I would have missed with Semrush alone.

Ahrefs also updates its index faster. When I earned a new backlink from a guest post, Ahrefs picked it up within 48 hours. Semrush took about a week. For active link builders who need to track campaign results quickly, that speed difference adds up.

Semrush's backlink tool isn't bad — it's actually very good. But "very good" sits next to "best in class," and that gap matters if backlinks are central to your strategy.

Site Auditing: Both Are Excellent, Different Focus

I ran site audits on all three test sites using both tools. Here's where things got interesting.

Semrush's Site Audit crawled faster and produced a cleaner report. It categorizes issues into Errors, Warnings, and Notices with color coding that makes it easy to prioritize. The tool also scores your site's overall health as a percentage, which is great for client reporting — "Your site health improved from 78% to 91% this quarter" is a sentence clients actually understand.

Ahrefs' Site Audit went deeper on technical issues. It caught JavaScript rendering problems, orphan pages, and redirect chains that Semrush missed on one of my test sites. The reports are more detailed but also more technical — less friendly for non-SEO stakeholders.

For client-facing SEO work, I reach for Semrush. For my own sites where I want the most thorough technical check, I use Ahrefs.

Content Tools: Semrush's Big Advantage

This is where Semrush pulls decisively ahead. Semrush has an entire content marketing toolkit built into the platform:

  • SEO Content Template — gives you a brief for any target keyword based on top-ranking pages
  • SEO Writing Assistant — a real-time editor that scores your content for SEO, readability, originality, and tone
  • Topic Research — generates subtopics, questions, and headlines around any theme
  • ContentShake AI — an AI writing tool built into the platform that generates draft articles

Ahrefs has a Content Explorer, which is good for finding popular content in your niche and identifying link-worthy topics. But it's a research tool, not a writing tool. There's no equivalent to Semrush's Writing Assistant or Content Template.

If content creation is a big part of your workflow — and for most SEO professionals in 2026, it is — Semrush's content suite is a legitimate selling point that Ahrefs doesn't match.

The pricing is remarkably similar. At the entry level, Ahrefs is $10/month cheaper. At mid-tier, they're essentially the same. The real cost difference comes from what's included at each tier.

Semrush Pro includes content marketing tools, social media posting, local SEO features, and advertising research. Ahrefs Lite is focused purely on SEO — keyword research, backlinks, rank tracking, and site auditing. If you're using all of Semrush's extras, it's arguably better value. If you only need core SEO features, you're paying for things you won't use.

The Learning Curve

Ahrefs is easier to learn. I've watched dozens of people use both tools for the first time, and Ahrefs consistently produces that "oh, I get it" moment faster. The interface is cleaner. The navigation is more intuitive. The data is presented without clutter.

Semrush has more features, which means more menus, more options, and more potential confusion. New users frequently tell me they feel lost in Semrush's dashboard. It takes a few weeks to really understand where everything lives and what all the numbers mean.

Both companies have excellent educational content. Ahrefs' blog and YouTube channel are legendary in the SEO world. Semrush Academy offers free certifications that look good on a resume. But for pure "pick it up and start using it" ease, Ahrefs wins.

What I Actually Use Day to Day

After three months of testing, here's where I landed:

  • Keyword research: Semrush (intent data is too useful to ignore)
  • Backlink analysis: Ahrefs (larger index, faster updates)
  • Site auditing: Both — Semrush for client reports, Ahrefs for deep technical dives
  • Content planning: Semrush (no contest — the content suite is excellent)
  • Competitor research: Semrush (more data points, advertising intelligence)
  • Rank tracking: Ahrefs (cleaner interface, more accurate daily tracking)

Yes, I use both. I know that's an annoying answer, but it's the truth. If I could only keep one, I'd keep Semrush because it covers more ground. But I'd miss Ahrefs' backlink data every single day.

🏆 The Verdict

Choose Semrush if you need an all-in-one platform that covers keyword research, content creation, social media, PPC research, and local SEO in a single subscription. It's the wider toolkit. Choose Ahrefs if backlink analysis is critical to your work, you prefer a cleaner interface, or you're primarily focused on core SEO tasks without the extras. If budget allows, using both gives you the most complete picture — but if you're picking one, match it to what you spend the most time doing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Semrush or Ahrefs better for keyword research?

Both are excellent. Semrush gives you more keyword suggestions per query and includes search intent labels. Ahrefs provides click-through data that shows actual clicking behavior. For most users, the difference is marginal — pick based on other features you need.

Which tool has a better backlink database?

Ahrefs. It has the largest backlink index in the industry and updates it more frequently. In my testing, Ahrefs found 15% more referring domains than Semrush for the same sites.

Can I use the free versions of Semrush and Ahrefs?

Semrush offers 10 free searches per day with limited data. Ahrefs has free Webmaster Tools for verified site owners. Neither free tier is enough for serious SEO work, but they're useful for getting a feel for each platform.

Is Semrush worth $139.95 per month?

If SEO drives real business results for you, yes. Semrush bundles tools that would cost much more separately — keyword research, rank tracking, site auditing, content optimization, and competitive analysis. For hobbyists, it's probably overkill.

Which tool is better for beginners?

Ahrefs. Its interface is cleaner, reports are more readable, and their educational content (blog, YouTube, Academy) is among the best in the industry. Semrush has more features but can feel overwhelming at first.
