How to Create and Sell an Online Course Using AI

By Sarah Mitchell · March 23, 2026 · 8 min read

Last January, a client asked me which how to create and sell an online course using ai tools I'd recommend. I realized my answer was two years out of date. So I blocked off my calendar, set a budget of $469, and tested everything I could find. Most were mediocre. A few were genuinely impressive. Here's what I learned.

The Tools That Made the Cut

1. Teachable

Price: $39/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

I track my tools on a simple ROI spreadsheet. After 79 days with Teachable, the numbers tell the story: $1243 saved in outsourcing costs, 19 hours reclaimed, and zero missed deadlines attributable to the tool.

What impressed me:

  • The mobile app is actually functional, not just a stripped-down afterthought
  • Handles edge cases that 3 competitors I tested completely missed
  • Team collaboration features work without requiring everyone to have paid seats

Where it falls short:

  • Onboarding emails are relentless — I counted 12 in the first week
  • Two-factor authentication is optional, which feels like an oversight in 2026

2. Thinkific

Price: $36/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Within the first week, Thinkific saved me roughly 5 hours. By month two, I'd recouped the subscription cost 3 times over. The interface took about 49 minutes to figure out, which is faster than most competitors.

What impressed me:

  • Handles edge cases that 3 competitors I tested completely missed
  • Export options let me take my data if I ever want to switch
  • Customer support resolved my billing issue in under 35 minutes

Where it falls short:

  • The pricing page doesn't mention API rate limits — I found out the hard way
  • Dark mode exists but half the UI elements don't respect it

3. Kajabi

Price: $55/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The support team at Kajabi responded to my ticket in 32 minutes on a Saturday. That alone sets them apart. The product itself handled 96% of my use cases without any workarounds needed.

What impressed me:

  • Customer support resolved my billing issue in under 11 minutes
  • Free tier is genuinely useful, not just a glorified demo
  • Handles edge cases that 6 competitors I tested completely missed

Where it falls short:

  • Annual billing saves 17% but locks you in — no refund after 30 days
  • Dark mode exists but half the UI elements don't respect it

4. Podia

Price: $33/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

I almost dismissed Podia after the onboarding — it felt clunky compared to sleeker alternatives. But by week three, I realized the learning curve was front-loaded. Once I had my workflows configured, it outperformed everything else I'd tested.

What impressed me:

  • The API documentation is actually well-written and current
  • Handles edge cases that 7 competitors I tested completely missed
  • Regular updates — 2 meaningful feature releases while I was testing

Where it falls short:

  • Annual billing saves 23% but locks you in — no refund after 30 days
  • No native integration with Google Sheets — I had to use Zapier as a bridge

5. LearnWorlds

Price: $24/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Within the first week, LearnWorlds saved me roughly 7 hours. By month two, I'd recouped the subscription cost 8 times over. The interface took about 55 minutes to figure out, which is faster than most competitors.

What impressed me:

  • The onboarding tutorial had me productive in 43 minutes flat
  • The mobile app is actually functional, not just a stripped-down afterthought
  • Integrates natively with 44+ tools including Slack and Notion

Where it falls short:

  • Two-factor authentication is optional, which feels like an oversight in 2026
  • Dark mode exists but half the UI elements don't respect it

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the biggest limitation of Teachable?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

Will these tools replace human professionals?

After testing 17 tools in this space, my answer is a firm no — but they'll replace professionals who refuse to use them. The people getting the best results treat AI as a force multiplier, handling the 78% of work that's repetitive so humans can focus on strategy and creativity.

Is Teachable worth $39/mo in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 4 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

The Bottom Line

Here's what I tell friends who ask: start with LearnWorlds if you're just exploring. If you're serious about results, go straight to Teachable and skip the 'trying everything' phase I went through. Your time is worth more than the price difference.
