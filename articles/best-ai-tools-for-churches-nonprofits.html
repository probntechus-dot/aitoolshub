A pastor I know spends 8 hours a week on tasks that have nothing to do with ministry. Formatting bulletins. Designing social media graphics. Writing the same thank-you emails to donors. Updating the church website. Managing volunteer schedules.

She went into ministry to help people, not to become a one-person marketing department. But with a congregation of 200 and a staff of three, that's the reality for most church leaders.

Here's the good news: AI tools have gotten ridiculously good at handling exactly this kind of work, and most of them are either free or deeply discounted for nonprofits. I spent the last month testing tools specifically through the lens of churches and small nonprofits — organizations with tight budgets, limited tech skills, and no time to learn complicated software.

These eight tools passed my test. They're practical, affordable, and you don't need a computer science degree to use them.

Why Churches and Nonprofits Need AI (More Than They Think)

Let me share a number that stuck with me. According to a 2025 Barna Group survey, 73% of pastors say administrative tasks are their biggest source of burnout. Not counseling. Not sermon prep. Paperwork and communications.

Nonprofits face the same squeeze. The average nonprofit executive director spends 25% of their time on fundraising communication, 15% on donor management, and 10% on reporting. That's half their week on tasks that AI can significantly speed up.

The objection I hear most: "Our congregation wouldn't be comfortable with AI." And honestly, they probably wouldn't be comfortable with AI writing sermons or handling pastoral care. But formatting a bulletin? Designing an Instagram graphic? Writing a donation receipt email? Nobody cares if AI helped with that. Nor should they.

💡 The Right Way to Think About It

AI handles the administrative stuff so that ministry leaders can spend more time on the work only humans can do — building relationships, counseling, teaching, and being present in people's lives.

1. Canva

Best for: Social media graphics, bulletins, event flyers, presentations
Pricing: Free tier · Free for nonprofits (Canva Pro, normally $120/yr)

If your church or nonprofit isn't using Canva yet, this is the single biggest upgrade you can make today. And with the nonprofit discount, you get the full Pro plan — including all AI features — for free.

Canva's Magic Design generates professional layouts from a text prompt. Type "Sunday worship service flyer, modern, blue and gold" and you get 8-10 options in seconds. The Magic Write feature drafts text for social posts, event descriptions, and announcements. Magic Eraser removes distracting elements from photos. Background Remover isolates subjects for clean graphics.

A children's ministry director I spoke with uses Canva to create all her weekly materials — parent handouts, classroom signs, social media posts, and event promotions. She said it used to take her 4-5 hours per week. With Canva's AI features, she's down to about 90 minutes.

Pro tip for churches: Set up a Brand Kit with your church colors, fonts, and logo. Every design you create will automatically match your church's visual identity. This is included free in the nonprofit plan.

Canva's AI features turn anyone into a decent graphic designer — even if your design skills are... limited. Photo: Hal Gatewood on Unsplash

2. ChatGPT

Best for: Writing drafts, brainstorming, research, grant proposals
Pricing: Free tier · Plus at $20/month

ChatGPT is the Swiss Army knife of AI tools, and it's especially useful for nonprofit work. The free tier is genuinely capable enough for most church and nonprofit needs.

Here's how churches are actually using it:

Sermon prep assistance. Not writing sermons — that's still the pastor's job. But ChatGPT can research biblical context, suggest relevant modern illustrations, create discussion questions for small groups, and even outline sermon series themes. One pastor told me it cuts his research time in half.

Communication drafts. Thank-you letters to donors, welcome emails for new members, volunteer appreciation messages, event announcements, annual report narratives. ChatGPT drafts these in minutes. You edit for your voice and personal touches, but the heavy lifting is done.

Grant writing. For nonprofits, this might be the highest-value use case. ChatGPT can draft grant proposals, create program descriptions, write impact metrics sections, and structure budgets. Several nonprofit directors I interviewed said it reduced their grant writing time by 40-60%. One secured a $50,000 grant with a proposal that ChatGPT helped draft.

Important note: Always edit AI-generated content. ChatGPT doesn't know your community, your congregation's story, or the nuances that make your organization unique. Use it as a starting point, not a finished product.

3. Tithely

Best for: Online giving, donor management, church websites
Pricing: Free for basic giving · ChurchOS from $49/month

Digital giving isn't optional anymore. Post-pandemic, 60% of church donations happen online. Tithely makes that process smooth and adds AI smarts to donor engagement.

The basic giving platform is free — donors can give via app, text, or web, and Tithely takes a small transaction fee (similar to any payment processor). What makes it stand out is the AI-powered donor insights in the paid ChurchOS plan.

Tithely's AI can predict donation patterns, identify donors who might be lapsing, and suggest the optimal time to send giving reminders. It also personalizes thank-you messages based on giving history. For a church that depends on donations to keep the lights on, these features directly impact the bottom line.

The church website builder is a nice bonus. It's not as flexible as Squarespace, but it's designed specifically for churches — with sermon archives, event calendars, and giving integration built in. No coding required.

Where it falls short: Transaction fees add up for larger churches. The ChurchOS platform is feature-rich but can feel overwhelming if you only need basic giving. And the website builder, while functional, has limited design options.

4. Planning Center

Best for: Service planning, volunteer scheduling, member management
Pricing: Free for small churches · From $14/month per app

Planning Center is the backbone software for thousands of churches, and for good reason. It handles the operational chaos that every growing church faces: who's playing drums this Sunday, which volunteers are available for the kids' ministry, and who's falling through the cracks in follow-up.

The AI additions are targeted and useful. Smart scheduling learns volunteer preferences and availability patterns, then auto-generates scheduling suggestions that respect people's boundaries. No more calling 15 people to fill one slot. Attendance prediction uses historical data to forecast how many people will show up on a given Sunday — useful for everything from seating to snack purchasing.

The People module (their member management tool) now includes AI-assisted follow-up suggestions. When someone visits for the first time, Planning Center suggests a follow-up sequence based on what's worked for similar visitors in the past. It's not rocket science, but it turns inconsistent follow-up into a reliable process.

Where it falls short: Planning Center is modular — you pay separately for each app (Services, People, Giving, Groups, etc.). For a church that needs everything, costs can add up to $100+ per month. The learning curve is also moderate; budget a few weeks for your team to get comfortable.

The point of these tools isn't technology — it's freeing up people to focus on people. Photo: Priscilla Du Preez on Unsplash

5. ProPresenter

Best for: Worship presentations, lyrics display, live streaming
Pricing: $399 one-time (ProPresenter 7) · Campus license from $1,299

ProPresenter is the standard presentation software for church worship services, and the recent AI features have made it even more essential.

The AI-powered lyric formatting automatically arranges song lyrics into readable slides with proper line breaks and verse/chorus labeling. If you've ever manually formatted 20 worship songs for a Sunday setlist, you know how much time this saves. The AI also suggests background images and motion graphics that match the song's mood.

Auto-captioning is another standout. ProPresenter can generate real-time captions for sermons and announcements, making services accessible to deaf and hard-of-hearing members. As an accessibility feature, this is significant — and it works without any additional hardware or services.

The one-time pricing is refreshing in a world of monthly subscriptions. You buy it once, and it works. Updates are included. For churches that are already spending $50-100/month on presentation software subscriptions, ProPresenter pays for itself in a few months.

Where it falls short: The upfront cost is significant for small churches. And ProPresenter runs best on Mac — the Windows version works but can be less stable. You'll also need a decent computer; this software is resource-intensive.

6. Subsplash

Best for: Church apps, media hosting, integrated digital platform
Pricing: Custom (typically $100-300/month)

Subsplash builds custom mobile apps and digital platforms for churches. Having your own app might sound like overkill for a small church, but the engagement numbers tell a different story.

Churches using Subsplash report that app users give 2-3x more frequently than non-app users. The AI-powered push notifications are a big part of why. Subsplash's AI determines the optimal time to send notifications to each user based on their engagement patterns. Instead of blasting everyone at 9 AM Saturday with a "join us tomorrow" message, each person gets the notification when they're most likely to read it.

The media platform is also excellent. Subsplash hosts sermon videos, podcasts, and devotional content with AI-powered recommendations. Members see content suggestions based on their viewing history — similar to how Netflix works, but for your church's content library.

Where it falls short: The price point puts it out of reach for many small churches. And honestly, if your church has fewer than 200 regular attendees, a good website with Tithely integration might serve you better than a dedicated app. The engagement benefits really kick in at scale.

7. Faithlife

Best for: Bible study, sermon research, church management
Pricing: Free tier · Faithlife Equip from $16.99/month

Faithlife is the company behind Logos Bible Software, which is the gold standard for serious biblical study. Their church platform extends that research depth into ministry management.

The AI-powered sermon research is where Faithlife really shines. It connects directly to the massive Logos library and can suggest relevant commentary, cross-references, original language insights, and historical context for any passage. For pastors who want depth in their teaching, this is incredibly valuable — it's like having a seminary library and a research assistant available 24/7.

Faithlife Groups include AI discussion guides that generate small group questions based on the week's sermon passage. Group leaders don't need to spend hours creating discussion material — the AI generates thoughtful, open-ended questions that actually spark conversation.

The church management features (member directory, church calendar, group communication) are solid but not as polished as Planning Center. Think of Faithlife as the choice for churches that prioritize teaching and Bible study, and Planning Center as the choice for churches that prioritize operational efficiency.

Where it falls short: The interface feels dated compared to newer tools. And the Logos Bible Software integration — while powerful — has a steep learning curve. New users can feel overwhelmed by the sheer volume of resources available.

Faithlife brings seminary-level research tools to pastors at every church size. Photo: Aaron Burden on Unsplash

8. Mailchimp

Best for: Email newsletters, donor communication, event promotion
Pricing: Free (up to 500 contacts) · Essentials from $13/month

Email is still the most effective way to communicate with your congregation and donors. Social media posts reach maybe 5% of your followers. Emails reach 90%+ of your list, with open rates for church newsletters averaging 35-45% — much higher than the industry average.

Mailchimp's AI features make email management remarkably easy. Content Optimizer analyzes your drafts and suggests improvements for clarity, engagement, and mobile readability. Send Time Optimization uses AI to deliver each email when the recipient is most likely to open it. Creative Assistant generates branded email designs from your website or social media presence.

The free tier is generous enough for small churches — 500 contacts with 1,000 sends per month. For a church of 100-150 members, that's plenty. And Mailchimp offers a 15% discount for verified nonprofits on paid plans.

Here's a practical workflow: After each Sunday service, send a newsletter with the sermon summary (drafted by ChatGPT), a designed header (created in Canva), upcoming events, and a giving link (via Tithely). With these AI tools working together, the whole process takes about 20 minutes.

Where it falls short: Mailchimp has gotten more expensive over the years, and the free tier has shrunk. If you have more than 500 contacts, you'll need a paid plan. Also, the automation features in the free plan are basic — you'll need at least the Standard plan ($20/month) for advanced automation.

Getting Started Without Overwhelm

Don't try to adopt all eight tools at once. That's a recipe for frustration and abandoned accounts. Here's a practical rollout plan:

Week 1-2: Start with Canva and ChatGPT. These are free, immediately useful, and don't require integration with anything else. Use ChatGPT to draft your weekly communications and Canva to design them. You'll see time savings within the first week.

Month 1: Add Mailchimp. Set up your contact list and start sending a consistent weekly newsletter. Use the ChatGPT + Canva workflow to create content, then send it through Mailchimp.

Month 2-3: Add a management platform. Choose either Planning Center (if operations are your pain point) or Tithely (if giving and donor engagement are the priority). Don't add both at once.

Month 4+: Evaluate specialty tools. Once your basics are solid, consider ProPresenter, Subsplash, or Faithlife based on your church's specific needs.

🔑 Budget Reality Check

The free stack: Canva (nonprofit plan) + ChatGPT (free tier) + Mailchimp (free tier) = $0/month. This covers design, writing, and email for a small church.

The full stack: Add Planning Center ($50/mo) + Tithely ChurchOS ($49/mo) + ProPresenter ($399 one-time) = roughly $120/month ongoing. Still less than a part-time admin assistant.

Frequently Asked Questions

What free AI tools can churches use?

Churches can use ChatGPT (free tier) for sermon prep, bulletin writing, and email drafts. Canva's free plan includes AI-powered design tools for social media and bulletins. Mailchimp's free tier handles email newsletters for up to 500 contacts. Many churches also qualify for Google Workspace for Nonprofits, which is free.

Is it ethical for churches to use AI?

Using AI as a tool for administrative tasks, communication, and outreach is widely accepted. Most church leaders draw the line at using AI to write sermons without disclosure or to replace genuine pastoral care. AI works best as an assistant that handles busywork so ministry leaders can focus on people.

Does Canva offer nonprofit discounts?

Yes — Canva offers Canva for Nonprofits, which provides free access to Canva Pro (normally $120/year) for eligible nonprofit organizations, including churches with 501(c)(3) status. This includes all AI-powered design features, brand kits, and premium templates.

What is the best church management software with AI?

Planning Center is the most widely used church management platform and has been adding AI features for attendance prediction and volunteer scheduling. Faithlife is the best option for churches that want integrated Bible study tools alongside management features. Tithely is best for churches focused on digital giving and donor engagement.

Can nonprofits use ChatGPT for grant writing?

Yes — ChatGPT is excellent for drafting grant proposals, creating program descriptions, and writing impact reports. It won't replace the need for accurate data and genuine storytelling, but it can significantly speed up the writing process. Many nonprofits report cutting grant writing time by 40-60% using AI assistance.
