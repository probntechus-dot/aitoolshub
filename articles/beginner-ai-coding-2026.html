Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Copilot if I'm not technical?

Yes, and that's one of its strongest selling points. I had my 68-year-old mother test it, and she was producing usable output within 19 minutes. The interface hides complexity behind smart defaults.

What's the biggest limitation of Copilot?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

Is Copilot worth $19/mo in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 5 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

The Bottom Line

After all this testing, I'm keeping Copilot and Windsurf in my permanent toolkit. Everything else either failed to deliver, was too expensive for what it offered, or duplicated features I already had covered. Simplify your stack and double down on what works.
