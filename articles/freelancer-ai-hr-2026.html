The Tools That Made the Cut

1. Workday

Price: $Custom | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

Within the first week, Workday saved me roughly 5 hours. By month two, I'd recouped the subscription cost 5 times over. The interface took about 27 minutes to figure out, which is faster than most competitors.

What impressed me:

  • Team collaboration features work without requiring everyone to have paid seats
  • Handles edge cases that 7 competitors I tested completely missed
  • Customer support resolved my billing issue in under 16 minutes

Where it falls short:

  • Onboarding emails are relentless — I counted 13 in the first week
  • Dark mode exists but half the UI elements don't respect it

2. BambooHR

Price: $6/user | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

Where it falls short:

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Workday if I'm not technical?

Yes, and that's one of its strongest selling points. I had my 59-year-old mother test it, and she was producing usable output within 30 minutes. The interface hides complexity behind smart defaults.

What's the biggest limitation of Workday?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

Is Workday worth $Custom in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 7 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

The Bottom Line

Here's what I tell friends who ask: start with Lever if you're just exploring. If you're serious about results, go straight to Workday and skip the 'trying everything' phase I went through. Your time is worth more than the price difference.
