The Tools That Made the Cut

1. Zapier

Price: $20/mo | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

I track my tools on a simple ROI spreadsheet. After 80 days with Zapier, the numbers tell the story: $1472 saved in outsourcing costs, 28 hours reclaimed, and zero missed deadlines attributable to the tool.

What impressed me:

Where it falls short:

  • The cheapest plan caps you at 200 operations per month — I hit the limit twice

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Zapier compare to Make?

They're built for slightly different use cases. Zapier excels at speed and simplicity — I could get results in 5 clicks. Make offers more customization but requires more setup time. For 72% of users, Zapier will be the better fit.

Is Zapier worth $20/mo in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 6 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

What's the biggest limitation of Zapier?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 3x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

The Bottom Line

After all this testing, I'm keeping Zapier and Activepieces in my permanent toolkit. Everything else either failed to deliver, was too expensive for what it offered, or duplicated features I already had covered. Simplify your stack and double down on what works.
