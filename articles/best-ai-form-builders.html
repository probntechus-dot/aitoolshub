AI Form Builders: Create Forms in Seconds (7 Tools Tested)

Building forms used to be one of those tasks that took 10 minutes to describe and 2 hours to actually do. You'd drag fields around, fiddle with conditional logic, test on mobile, realize the layout broke, fix it, test again — and by the time you published, you'd lost the will to live.

Then AI form builders showed up and said: "Just tell me what you want."

I tested 7 AI-powered form builders with the same prompt: "Create a customer feedback form for an online clothing store, including questions about product quality, delivery experience, and likelihood to recommend." Here's what each tool produced, how much customization was needed, and whether the AI actually saved time or just created new problems.

How I Tested Each Tool

For consistency, I used the same evaluation criteria across all 7 tools:

  • AI quality: How good was the form straight from the AI prompt? Could I publish it as-is?
  • Customization: How easy was it to tweak fields, add logic, and change the design?
  • Integrations: Does it connect to the tools I already use (Sheets, Slack, Notion, CRMs)?
  • Pricing: What do you actually pay for real-world usage?
  • Unique factor: What does this tool do that others don't?

Let's get into it.

1. Tally — Best Free AI Form Builder (No Contest)

I'll cut to the chase: Tally is the form builder I recommend most often, and their AI generation feature is a big reason why.

When I entered my test prompt, Tally generated a clean, well-structured form in about 8 seconds. It included the right field types (multiple choice for product quality, rating scale for delivery, NPS-style for recommendation), and even added a text area for open-ended feedback that I didn't specifically ask for. Smart.

But here's what makes Tally special: their free plan includes unlimited forms and unlimited responses. That's not a typo. Most competitors cap you at 100 responses or 10 forms on free plans. Tally doesn't. The paid plan ($29/month) adds custom domains, team features, and branding removal — but for solo creators and small teams, the free tier is genuinely sufficient.

The editor works like a document — you type and add fields inline, rather than dragging blocks around. It feels more like writing in Notion than using a traditional form builder. That approach won't suit everyone, but I found it much faster than grid-based editors.

AI quality: 8.5/10 — Minimal edits needed.
Free plan: Unlimited forms + unlimited responses.
Paid: From $29/month.
Best for: Anyone who wants powerful forms without paying anything.

2. Typeform — The Beautiful One

Typeform has always been the form builder you choose when design matters. Their one-question-at-a-time interface creates an experience that feels more like a conversation than a form, and response rates are typically 20-30% higher than traditional multi-field forms.

Their AI form generation is solid. My test prompt produced a visually appealing, conversational-style form with appropriate question types and smooth transitions. The AI also suggested a welcome screen and thank-you page with copy that felt on-brand for an e-commerce store.

The downside? Typeform's free plan is extremely limited (10 responses/month). For any real use, you're looking at $25-50/month. But if form completion rates matter to your business — and they should, especially for feedback and lead generation — Typeform's conversion advantage can justify the price.

AI quality: 8/10 — Great design, good question flow.
Free plan: 10 responses/month (very limited).
Paid: From $25/month (Basic) to $50/month (Plus).
Best for: Customer-facing forms where design and completion rates matter.

3. Jotform — The Feature-Packed Veteran

Jotform has been around forever, and their form builder has more features than some CRMs. With 10,000+ templates, 100+ integrations, payment processing, e-signatures, PDF generation, and now AI — Jotform is the Swiss Army knife of form builders.

Their AI form generation worked well for my test prompt, producing a comprehensive form with conditional logic already built in (if rating is below 3, ask "What went wrong?"). That conditional logic suggestion was the best of any tool I tested — most AI builders generate flat forms without branching.

Jotform's free plan allows 5 forms with 100 submissions/month, which is workable for testing but tight for production. The paid plans start at $34/month and include more forms, submissions, and storage.

AI quality: 8/10 — Particularly strong on conditional logic.
Free plan: 5 forms, 100 submissions/month.
Paid: From $34/month.
Best for: Complex forms with advanced logic, payments, and integrations.

4. Fillout — The Notion-Native Form Builder

Fillout is the new kid on the block that's gaining traction fast, especially among Notion and Airtable users. It connects directly to your databases, so form responses automatically populate your existing workspace — no Zapier required.

The AI form generation produced a clean, modern form from my test prompt. Nothing flashy, but well-organized and mobile-friendly. What I appreciated was how quickly I could connect it to a Notion database and have responses flow in as database entries with proper properties.

Fillout also supports calculations, scheduling (like embedded Calendly-style booking), and multi-page forms with progress bars. The free plan includes unlimited forms with 1,000 responses/month — very generous for a newer tool.

AI quality: 7.5/10 — Clean and functional, not flashy.
Free plan: Unlimited forms, 1,000 responses/month.
Paid: From $19/month.
Best for: Teams using Notion or Airtable as their backend.

5. Formless (by Typeform) — Conversational AI That Asks Follow-Ups

Formless is Typeform's experimental sibling, and it does something genuinely different from every other tool on this list: instead of generating a static form, it creates an AI-powered conversation that asks follow-up questions based on user responses.

For my test, I didn't get a traditional form at all. Instead, Formless created a chat-style interface where the AI asks about product quality, listens to the response, and dynamically asks relevant follow-ups. If a customer mentions a sizing issue, the AI asks about specific products. If delivery was late, it digs into timing details.

This approach collects richer data than checkboxes ever could. But it's not for every use case — sometimes people just want to check a few boxes and move on. For feedback collection, customer research, and user interviews, though, Formless is fascinating.

AI quality: 9/10 — But it's a different category entirely.
Free plan: Limited conversations.
Paid: From $35/month.
Best for: Qualitative research, customer discovery, detailed feedback collection.

6. MakeForms — Enterprise-Grade With AI Simplicity

MakeForms targets businesses that need forms with compliance features — HIPAA, GDPR, SOC 2, data residency controls. The AI generation feature works similarly to other tools, but the output includes compliance-appropriate consent checkboxes, data handling notices, and field-level encryption options.

For my clothing store test, the AI-generated form was perfectly functional but noticeably more "corporate" in tone than Tally or Typeform's output. That's fine for B2B use cases (employee onboarding forms, compliance surveys, vendor assessments) but slightly stiff for consumer-facing applications.

Where MakeForms earns its spot is in regulated industries. If you need HIPAA-compliant patient intake forms, GDPR-compliant data collection for EU customers, or audit trails for every submission, MakeForms handles that out of the box.

AI quality: 7/10 — Functional but corporate in tone.
Free plan: Limited; mainly for testing.
Paid: From $29/month.
Best for: Regulated industries needing HIPAA/GDPR compliance.

7. OpnForm — The Open-Source Option

OpnForm is the open-source alternative on this list. You can self-host it for free (if you have the technical chops) or use their hosted version with a generous free plan.

The AI form generation is respectable — my test produced a usable form with the right field types, though the design was more utilitarian than Tally or Typeform. The real value of OpnForm is ownership: your data stays on your servers, you can customize the code however you want, and there are no per-response limits.

For developers and tech teams, OpnForm is a no-brainer. For non-technical users, the hosted version works fine but doesn't match the polish of commercial alternatives. Think of it as the WordPress of form builders — powerful and flexible, but you trade some convenience for control.

AI quality: 6.5/10 — Functional but less polished.
Free plan: Unlimited (self-hosted) or generous hosted tier.
Paid: Open-source; hosted plans from $25/month.
Best for: Developers who want full control and data ownership.

The Verdict: Which AI Form Builder Should You Use?

After testing all seven with the same prompt and evaluating the results, here's my honest recommendation:

  • Best overall (free): Tally. Unlimited everything on the free plan, great AI output, clean editor. Hard to beat.
  • Best for conversions: Typeform. Higher completion rates justify the premium price for customer-facing forms.
  • Best for complex logic: Jotform. If your form has 15+ fields with branching logic, Jotform handles it best.
  • Best for Notion/Airtable users: Fillout. Native database integration saves hours of Zapier wrangling.
  • Most innovative: Formless. Conversational AI that asks smart follow-ups. Different from everything else.
  • Best for compliance: MakeForms. HIPAA, GDPR, and audit trails built in.
  • Best for developers: OpnForm. Open-source, self-hostable, fully customizable.

The AI form building space is moving fast, and honestly, the quality gap between these tools is shrinking. The real differentiators are pricing models, integration ecosystems, and design philosophy. Pick the one that fits your workflow, not the one with the most features you'll never use.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI form builder?

An AI form builder is a tool that uses artificial intelligence to generate form fields, logic, and layouts from a simple text description. Instead of manually dragging and dropping fields, you describe what you need ("create a customer feedback form for a restaurant") and the AI builds it for you in seconds.

Are AI-generated forms as good as manually built ones?

For standard use cases (surveys, feedback, registration, contact forms), AI-generated forms are excellent and often include fields you might have forgotten. For complex forms with advanced conditional logic or custom integrations, you'll likely need to tweak the AI output. Think of AI as a fast first draft, not a finished product.

Which AI form builder is completely free?

Tally offers unlimited forms and responses on their free plan — no hidden limits. OpnForm is open-source and free for self-hosting. Most other tools (Typeform, Jotform, Fillout) have free tiers with some limitations on responses or features.

Can AI form builders handle payment collection?

Yes. Tally, Typeform, Jotform, and Fillout all support payment collection through Stripe integration. Jotform also supports PayPal and Square. You can build order forms, donation pages, and event registration with payments built in.

Do AI form builders integrate with other tools?

All major AI form builders integrate with popular tools like Google Sheets, Slack, Notion, Airtable, HubSpot, and Zapier. Jotform has the widest native integration library (100+), while tools like Tally and Fillout rely more on Zapier and Make for third-party connections.
