AI Last-Mile Delivery Optimization Tools for 2026

By Sarah Mitchell March 5, 2026 Updated: March 13, 2026 14 min read

The dispatcher at a meal-kit delivery company in Portland was manually building routes for 14 drivers every morning using Google Maps and a whiteboard. Each morning took 90 minutes. On the day I introduced her to Routific, she uploaded the same 280 delivery addresses and watched the algorithm generate optimized routes for all 14 drivers in 37 seconds. The resulting routes were 23% shorter in total distance than her manually planned ones. She turned to me and said something I've heard variations of at every company I've tested delivery software with: "Why didn't we do this two years ago?"

Last-mile delivery — the final leg from a distribution point to the customer's door — accounts for up to 53% of total shipping costs according to industry estimates. That staggering proportion makes it the single largest opportunity for logistics cost reduction, and AI-powered optimization tools have matured to the point where even small delivery operations can capture meaningful savings. I spent six weeks testing seven platforms across real delivery operations ranging from 50 to 15,000 daily stops.

Routific — Best for Growing Delivery Businesses

Routific has built what I consider the most accessible entry point into AI-powered delivery optimization. The Vancouver-based company offers a genuinely useful free tier that supports up to 100 orders per month — enough for a small bakery, florist, or artisan food producer to experience route optimization without spending a dollar. When volume grows beyond that, their paid plan starts at $150 per month for up to 1,000 orders, with additional usage priced per order and volume discounts for higher volumes.

During my testing with the meal-kit company, Routific's optimization engine consistently produced routes that were 18-25% more efficient than manual planning in terms of total distance driven. The platform considers time windows, vehicle capacity constraints, driver breaks, and traffic patterns when building routes. Uploading orders via CSV took seconds, and the drag-and-drop interface for manual adjustments felt polished and responsive.

The driver app sends customers automated ETA notifications as the driver progresses through their route, and the real-time tracking dashboard gave dispatchers visibility into each driver's position and remaining stops. Proof of delivery captures photos and signatures, creating an audit trail that the company's customer service team found invaluable for resolving "I didn't receive my delivery" disputes.

Where Routific falls short is dynamic re-routing. Once a route is dispatched, adding new stops mid-route requires manual intervention. For operations with same-day or on-demand delivery components, this limitation becomes significant. The platform is purpose-built for planned routes — tomorrow's deliveries optimized today — and it excels at that specific workflow.

OptimoRoute — Flexible Per-Driver Pricing

OptimoRoute addresses a different pricing anxiety: the fear of unpredictable costs as your operation scales. Their per-driver pricing model — $35.10 per driver per month for the Starter plan and $49 per driver per month for the Pro plan — means you always know exactly what your software costs will be, regardless of order volume. A 5-driver operation pays $245 per month on the Pro plan, period.

I tested OptimoRoute at a home healthcare supplies delivery company running 8 drivers across the greater Denver metro area. The platform's standout feature was its handling of complex constraints. Each delivery had a specific time window negotiated with the patient, certain drivers were certified for specific equipment types, and vehicle weight limits varied across the fleet. OptimoRoute balanced all these constraints simultaneously, producing routes that the lead dispatcher described as "better than anything I could plan in four hours, and it took four minutes."

The Pro plan adds real-time order tracking, breadcrumb trails showing actual routes versus planned routes, and five weeks of scheduling horizon — meaning you can plan and optimize delivery schedules up to 35 days in advance. For operations with recurring delivery schedules, like weekly subscription deliveries or regular B2B supply runs, the long planning horizon enables strategic optimization that single-day tools can't match.

OptimoRoute lacks barcode scanning and built-in order management, which means it works best when paired with a separate order management system that feeds delivery data into OptimoRoute via CSV upload or API. The API documentation is thorough, and the healthcare company's IT team integrated it with their ERP system in under two weeks.

Onfleet — Real-Time Dispatch for Urban Operations

Onfleet targets urban delivery operations that need real-time dispatching, live customer tracking, and the flexibility to handle on-demand orders alongside scheduled routes. Their platform is built for speed — both in optimization calculation time and in the user experience for dispatchers managing fast-paced operations.

I evaluated Onfleet at a grocery delivery startup in Chicago processing 400-600 deliveries daily with a mixed fleet of employee drivers and gig workers. The auto-dispatch feature assigned incoming orders to the nearest available driver within seconds, factoring in current location, remaining capacity, and estimated completion time for active deliveries. During a lunch rush where 45 orders arrived within 20 minutes, the system distributed them across 12 drivers without any manual intervention from the dispatch team.

Onfleet's pricing reflects its enterprise positioning. The Launch plan starts at approximately $599 per month, includes route optimization, auto-dispatch, and extended reporting for growing operations. The Scale plan runs roughly $1,099 per month with advanced analytics and priority support. Enterprise pricing starts around $2,999 per month and includes multi-region support, dedicated account management, and enterprise security features. There's no free tier, which puts it out of reach for very small operations.

The customer-facing tracking experience is Onfleet's strongest differentiator at the consumer touchpoint. Recipients receive a branded tracking link showing their driver's location on a live map with an accurate ETA that updates dynamically. The grocery startup reported that customer service calls about delivery status dropped by 67% after implementing Onfleet's tracking notifications, freeing their support team to handle actual issues rather than answering "where's my order?" questions.

Locus.sh — Enterprise-Grade All-Mile Optimization

Locus positions itself as an end-to-end logistics optimization platform, covering first-mile pickup, mid-mile linehaul, and last-mile delivery within a single system. For large-scale operations managing thousands of daily deliveries across multiple cities or countries, Locus delivers a level of optimization sophistication that the smaller platforms simply cannot match.

I reviewed Locus's deployment at a national e-commerce fulfillment operation processing 15,000 daily deliveries across 12 Indian cities. The platform's route optimization engine considered factors that simpler tools ignore: historical traffic patterns at 15-minute intervals, loading dock availability at distribution centers, vehicle turn restrictions on narrow urban streets, and even the probability of delivery failure at specific addresses based on past attempts.

Locus pricing is enterprise-custom, negotiated based on daily delivery volume, geographic coverage, and feature requirements. Operations I spoke with reported monthly costs ranging from $3,000 to $12,000 depending on scale, with the per-delivery cost decreasing significantly at higher volumes. The platform requires dedicated implementation, typically taking 4-8 weeks for full deployment including integration with existing warehouse management and order management systems.

The ROI data from Locus deployments is compelling. The e-commerce operation reported a 14% reduction in fuel costs, a 21% increase in deliveries per vehicle per day, and a 34% reduction in failed first-attempt deliveries through AI-predicted optimal time slots. Their cost per delivery dropped from ₹52 to ₹38 — a 27% reduction that translated to savings exceeding $200,000 monthly across their network.

FarEye — Orchestrating Complex Delivery Networks

FarEye approaches last-mile delivery as an orchestration challenge rather than purely a routing problem. The platform manages the interplay between carriers, warehouses, delivery personnel, and customers through a unified command center that provides visibility and control across the entire delivery ecosystem.

Where FarEye distinguished itself during my evaluation was in managing hybrid delivery networks. A furniture retailer I observed used FarEye to coordinate deliveries across their own fleet for metro areas, third-party carriers for suburban zones, and white-glove delivery partners for premium customers — all managed through a single dashboard with consistent tracking and customer communication regardless of which carrier handled the actual delivery.

FarEye's pricing is enterprise-oriented, with plans typically starting around $1,500 per month and scaling based on shipment volume and feature modules. The platform integrates with over 200 carriers globally, making it particularly valuable for retailers and e-commerce companies that work with multiple delivery partners and need a unified layer of intelligence across all of them.

Wodely — Budget-Friendly Newcomer

Wodely represents the new generation of delivery management platforms that pack surprisingly capable optimization into affordable packages. Starting at just $49 per month, Wodely offers intelligent route optimization, live dashboards, automated customer notifications, and integrations with over 50 third-party tools. For micro-businesses running 1-5 drivers, the economics are hard to argue with.

I tested Wodely with a four-driver flower delivery service in Seattle during Valentine's week — arguably the most demanding week in their calendar. The platform handled 380 time-sensitive deliveries across four days, respecting tight delivery windows (most customers specified 2-hour windows) while keeping routes efficient. Total driving distance was 12% lower than the owner's previous manual routing, and all but 6 deliveries landed within their specified windows.

The limitations at this price point are predictable: the optimization engine handles fewer constraints than OptimoRoute or Locus, the analytics are basic, and there's no API access on the starter plan. For operations that need multi-depot routing, complex vehicle capacity constraints, or enterprise integrations, Wodely isn't the right tool. But for local delivery businesses that currently route manually or with basic mapping tools, Wodely delivers genuine AI optimization at a price point that previously only bought spreadsheet-level software.

Shipday — Free Tier for Micro-Operations

Shipday deserves mention for offering a genuinely functional free tier that includes up to 300 orders per month. For restaurants, bakeries, and small retailers doing their own local delivery, Shipday provides route optimization, driver tracking, customer notifications, and proof of delivery at zero cost until you outgrow the free allocation.

I observed a taco restaurant chain with three locations use Shipday to manage lunch and dinner delivery routes for their two in-house drivers. The platform organized each batch of orders into optimized routes, sent customers tracking links via text message, and collected delivery confirmations through the driver app. The owner estimated he was saving 45 minutes of daily planning time and had received zero customer complaints about missing orders since implementing the photo proof of delivery.

Beyond 300 monthly orders, Shipday's paid plans are competitively priced against Routific, though the optimization engine is less sophisticated in handling complex constraints. For pure restaurant and food delivery use cases, Shipday's food-specific features — including integration with Toast, Square, and other restaurant POS systems — make it a natural fit.

Making the Right Choice for Your Operation

The decision tree I recommend after testing all seven platforms starts with daily volume. Under 100 orders: start with Routific's free tier or Shipday. Between 100-1,000 orders: Routific at $150/month or OptimoRoute at $35-$49 per driver deliver the best value. For 1,000-5,000 daily orders with dynamic dispatch needs: Onfleet's capabilities justify its higher price point. Above 5,000 daily deliveries: Locus and FarEye provide the enterprise-grade optimization and orchestration that high-volume operations demand.

Every platform I tested reduced delivery costs compared to manual routing. The variation was in magnitude — from 12% savings with basic tools on simple routes to 40% savings with sophisticated platforms on complex multi-constraint delivery networks. AI route optimization has crossed the threshold from luxury to necessity for any delivery operation competing on cost and customer experience in 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best last-mile delivery optimization software for small businesses?

Routific offers the best entry point for small delivery businesses. Their free tier supports up to 100 orders per month, and the paid plan starts at $150 per month for up to 1,000 orders. The interface is intuitive enough that dispatchers can learn it in a single afternoon without technical training.

How much does AI route optimization software cost?

Pricing varies widely by scale. Routific starts at $150/month for up to 1,000 orders. OptimoRoute costs $35-$49 per driver per month. Onfleet's Launch plan starts at $599/month. Enterprise platforms like Locus.sh and FarEye provide custom pricing typically ranging from $2,000-$10,000+ monthly for large-scale operations.

How much can AI reduce last-mile delivery costs?

AI-powered route optimization typically reduces delivery costs by 20-40% through more efficient routing, reduced fuel consumption, and increased stops per driver per day. Companies using platforms like Locus report fuel savings of 12-18% and capacity increases of 15-25% with the same fleet size.

Does AI delivery software work for same-day delivery?

Yes. Platforms like Onfleet and Locus support dynamic same-day delivery with real-time route re-optimization as new orders arrive. These systems can insert new stops into existing routes within seconds, adjusting driver assignments and ETAs automatically to accommodate rush orders without disrupting scheduled deliveries.

What features should I look for in last-mile delivery software?

Essential features include AI route optimization, real-time driver tracking, proof of delivery (photo and signature), customer notification with live ETAs, and analytics dashboards. For growing operations, also evaluate API integration capabilities, multi-depot support, capacity constraint handling, and dynamic re-routing as your volume scales.
