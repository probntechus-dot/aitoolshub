Midjourney vs DALL-E 3: I Generated 300+ Business Images to Find Out Which AI Wins

LC
Lisa Chen
Brand Designer & AI Art Enthusiast
üìÖ January 2024 ‚è± 12 min read

I'm a brand designer who's been using AI image generators for the past two years. When Midjourney V6 dropped and DALL-E 3 was integrated into ChatGPT, I knew I needed to run a proper comparison for business use specifically.

This isn't a comparison of which makes prettier abstract art. This is about which tool works for marketing images, product mockups, social media visuals, and presentation graphics ‚Äî the real-world stuff businesses actually need.

I generated over 300 images across both platforms with identical prompts. Here's what I found.

üéØ Short Version

Midjourney wins on visual quality, style, and creative direction. DALL-E 3 wins on text rendering, following exact instructions, and accessibility. Most businesses should start with DALL-E 3 (it's easier) and graduate to Midjourney when quality becomes a priority.

The Key Difference Nobody Talks About

Midjourney is a style machine. You give it creative direction and it produces images that look genuinely professional and aesthetically considered. The results often surprise you in good ways ‚Äî it interprets your prompt with a creative intelligence that produces things you didn't quite imagine but immediately recognize as better than what you pictured.

DALL-E 3 is a precision machine. You describe exactly what you want and it produces exactly that. The surprise factor is low, but the control is high. If you need a specific scene with specific elements positioned in specific ways, DALL-E 3 outperforms Midjourney reliably.

For business use, this distinction matters a lot depending on what you're making.

Text in Images: DALL-E 3 Wins Decisively

If you need readable text in your images ‚Äî social media graphics with quotes, infographic-style images, product images with taglines ‚Äî DALL-E 3 is not even close. Midjourney still struggles with text rendering, producing garbled letters or convincingly wrong-looking words that require fixing in another tool.

DALL-E 3 renders text cleanly and accurately most of the time. Not perfectly, but well enough that you can use many outputs directly without post-editing.

For social media graphics, this is often a dealbreaker in DALL-E's favor.

Pure Visual Quality: Midjourney Wins

When both tools are asked to create a scene without text ‚Äî say, a hero image for a website, a mood board element, or an abstract representation of a concept ‚Äî Midjourney's output quality is noticeably higher.

The lighting, composition, and overall "professional photography" feel of Midjourney V6 outputs is genuinely remarkable. I've shown clients Midjourney outputs and they've asked whether I hired a photographer. I've never had that reaction to a DALL-E image.

For brand photography replacement, editorial visuals, and anything where aesthetic quality is paramount: Midjourney.

Ease of Use: DALL-E 3 Wins

Midjourney's Discord-based interface is an acquired taste. It works, but it's not intuitive for beginners. You're typing prompts into a chat interface, using parameter flags like --ar 16:9 and --v 6, and navigating a crowded Discord server. The learning curve is real.

DALL-E 3 lives inside ChatGPT. You describe what you want in plain English, ChatGPT often helps refine your request before sending it to DALL-E, and you see the result. That's it. No parameters, no Discord, no learning curve.

For non-designers or anyone who just needs to get something done quickly: DALL-E 3 is dramatically easier.

My Business-Specific Use Case Breakdown

Blog hero images: Midjourney wins. The quality is visually more compelling and drives more clicks.

Social media graphics with text: DALL-E 3 wins. Text rendering makes it practical, Midjourney outputs need Canva post-processing.

Product mockups and lifestyle shots: Midjourney wins. The photorealism is better for products in context.

Explainer and diagram-style images: DALL-E 3 wins. It follows specific layout instructions better.

Team/people photos: Neither is great, honestly. Both struggle with realistic, natural-looking people. For this, use actual photos.

The Third Option Worth Mentioning: Canva AI

Before you subscribe to either, check if your Canva Pro subscription includes AI image generation ‚Äî it does. Canva's Magic Media feature is built on stable diffusion and while it doesn't match Midjourney's quality, it's integrated directly into your design workflow. For many small business users, this is the right starting point.

My Final Recommendation

If you're just starting out: Use DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT Plus). Lower learning curve, good enough quality for most needs, effectively free if you're already a ChatGPT user.

If quality is a priority and you create a lot of visual content: Add Midjourney's Standard plan ($30/month). The quality jump is real and noticeable in your content performance.

If you're a designer or creative team: Use both. They complement each other. DALL-E for precision, Midjourney for artistry.

üí° Bottom Line

For most small businesses, DALL-E 3 through ChatGPT Plus is the right starting point ‚Äî it's cheaper, easier, and good enough for most use cases. Midjourney is a worthy upgrade when you're ready to invest in visual quality and are willing to learn its workflow. Don't subscribe to Midjourney if you haven't mastered DALL-E 3 yet.
