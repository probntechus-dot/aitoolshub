ChatGPT vs Claude: 60 Days of Real Business Testing ‚Äî Here's What I Found

SR
Sarah Reynolds
Operations Manager & AI Tester
üìÖ February 2024 ‚è± 13 min read

I'll admit something: I came into this comparison with a bias. I'd been using ChatGPT since it launched and I was pretty convinced Claude was just a copycat trying to grab some market share.

Sixty days later, my opinion is... complicated. In a good way.

I run operations for a mid-sized consulting firm, and I convinced my boss to let me formally test both AI tools across our actual work tasks for two months. I tracked time savings, output quality, and frustration levels (yes, I made a spreadsheet). Here's the full honest breakdown.

üèÜ Spoiler: There's No Clear Winner

ChatGPT is better for some things. Claude is genuinely superior for others. The right answer for most businesses is probably both ‚Äî but if you have to pick one, I'll tell you which it should be based on your specific use case.

The Test Setup

I used both tools on identical real tasks every week. No cherry-picking prompts. The tasks included:

  • Writing and editing client proposals and reports
  • Summarizing long documents (PDFs, meeting transcripts)
  • Answering complex questions about our industry
  • Brainstorming marketing angles
  • Drafting and replying to professional emails
  • Analyzing data and drawing insights
  • Writing code snippets (for our Excel macros and basic automation)

Total tasks tested: 312. Yes, I'm that obsessive about data.

Writing & Editing: Claude Wins (Barely)

This surprised me the most. Claude's writing quality is different from ChatGPT's in a way that's hard to describe but easy to feel. It's more... considered? Less performative?

When I asked both tools to write a consulting proposal intro, ChatGPT gave me something polished and competent. Claude gave me something that sounded like an actual human thought about the client's situation. Not dramatically better, but noticeably more nuanced.

Where Claude really shines is editing. I'll paste a draft I've written and ask Claude to improve it while maintaining my voice. ChatGPT tends to rewrite everything in its own style. Claude actually preserves what I wrote and just makes it better. Huge difference.

"Claude edits like a thoughtful colleague. ChatGPT edits like a well-meaning stranger who wants to help but doesn't know you." ‚Äî My actual note from week 3 of testing.

Research & Analysis: ChatGPT Wins (with Browsing)

Here's where ChatGPT's browsing capability becomes a dealbreaker. Claude's knowledge cuts off and it can't access the internet (at least not in the base version). For anything requiring current information ‚Äî recent industry reports, what competitors announced last quarter, today's pricing ‚Äî ChatGPT with browsing is simply in a different league.

I asked both tools to summarize the competitive landscape in our consulting niche. ChatGPT found recent reports, news articles, and gave me a current picture. Claude gave me an intelligent answer based on older information. Both helpful, but only one was actually useful for the proposal I was writing.

Long Documents: Claude Wins Easily

This is where Claude absolutely dominates. Its context window is enormous. I can paste a 50-page industry report and ask it detailed questions throughout our conversation. ChatGPT Plus handles decent context but starts to "forget" earlier parts of long conversations.

One practical example: I pasted our entire 80-page employee handbook and asked Claude to identify policies that conflicted with our actual practices. It found six issues I wasn't aware of. This would have taken a paralegal half a day. Claude did it in 4 minutes.

For legal documents, long contracts, research reports ‚Äî Claude isn't just better, it's in a completely different category.

Coding & Technical Tasks: ChatGPT Wins

Neither of us are developers, but our team uses a lot of Excel macros and we occasionally need simple Python scripts. ChatGPT's code quality is consistently better in our testing. It also debugs more effectively ‚Äî when I paste an error message, ChatGPT is more likely to actually fix the root problem rather than suggest surface-level changes.

The Personality Factor

This sounds weird, but hear me out. Claude has a personality. It will push back on requests it thinks are misguided (politely). It will tell you when it thinks you're approaching a problem the wrong way.

ChatGPT is more obedient. Sometimes that's what you want. Other times, you actually need someone to tell you your idea isn't as good as you think.

I had a funny moment in week 6 where I asked Claude to help me write a slightly aggressive email to a vendor. It helped, but added "I want to flag that this tone might damage a relationship that could be valuable ‚Äî here's an alternative that's still firm but less likely to burn bridges." The alternative was better. I used it.

My Final Recommendation

After 60 days and 312 tasks, here's my honest take:

Choose ChatGPT if: You need web browsing, you do a lot of coding, or you prefer a more obedient assistant that executes your instructions without questioning them.

Choose Claude if: You work with long documents, you want better editing of your own writing, or you value an AI that will challenge your thinking.

Use both if: You're serious about getting the best possible output and $40/month is within reach. I use ChatGPT in the morning for research and Claude in the afternoon for writing and editing. Weird workflow, but it works.

üí° The Bottom Line

ChatGPT is the more versatile daily driver. Claude is the better writer and analyst. Neither is definitively "best" ‚Äî the right choice depends on your primary use case. For most small businesses, ChatGPT Plus is the safe default. But if you work heavily with documents and writing, Claude might surprise you.
