Superhuman: Fast But Expensive

Superhuman is the fastest email client ever made. Keyboard shortcuts, AI drafting, smart search—everything is optimized for speed.

Time saved: 30-45 minutes daily on email processing.

Cost: $30/month ($360/year)

Question: Is 30 minutes/day worth $360/year? If you bill hourly, probably yes. If not, maybe not.

Gmail Gemini: Free and Surprisingly Good

Gmail's new AI features are legitimately useful. Draft generation, smart reply, summarization—all free with Gmail.

Best use: Drafting responses to routine emails. Cuts time significantly.

Limitation: Requires Google Workspace. Not available in personal Gmail (yet).

Honest take: For 90% of people, Gmail Gemini is enough. Superhuman is for people obsessed with email optimization.

Outlook Copilot: Microsoft's Answer

If you use Microsoft 365, Copilot in Outlook is solid. Draft suggestions, meeting summaries, priority inbox ranking.

Cost: Included with Microsoft 365 (or $20/month for Copilot Pro)

Best for: Enterprise users already in Microsoft ecosystem.

Mailbutler: The Hidden Gem

Mailbutler adds AI to Gmail at $10/month. Less flashy than Superhuman but effective.

Features: Email templates, writing assistance, follow-up reminders.

My Recommendation

Personal Gmail user: Use Gmail Gemini (free)

Google Workspace: Gmail Gemini + Perplexity Pro for research = $20/month

Microsoft 365: Outlook Copilot (included)

Email power user: Superhuman ($30/month) if processing email is core to your job
