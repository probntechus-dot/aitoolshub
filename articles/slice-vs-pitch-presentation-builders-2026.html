Slice vs Pitch: AI Presentation Builders Comparison 2026

⏱ 4 min read
By Sarah Mitchell | March 3, 2026 | 8 min read

Bottom Line

Pitch for speed and polish. Slice for customization and brand control. Both are exceptional AI-powered presentation builders in 2026. I created 10 different decks in each to compare real-world experience.

Key Finding: Pitch creates presentation-ready decks in 5-10 minutes. Slice takes longer but gives more control over every element. Choose based on deadline vs. customization needs.

Pitch: The Fast & Beautiful Option

Pitch has become the go-to for founders and sales teams who need professional decks quickly. Give it a prompt, it generates a full deck with visuals.

Pitch strengths:

  • AI Generation: Describe your presentation, AI creates slides with content and design. I tested: "Series A pitch deck" generated 20 slides in 8 minutes.
  • Template Library: 100+ professional templates for every use case.
  • Real-Time Collaboration: Multiple people can edit simultaneously.
  • Brand Kits: Ensure all decks match your brand colors and fonts.
  • Presentation Mode: Speaker notes, presenter view, and animations built-in.
  • Export: PDF, PowerPoint, or share link directly.
  • Pricing: Free tier available, Pro at $13/month.

Pitch's weakness: less customization than PowerPoint. If you need pixel-perfect control, Pitch can feel limiting. Also, AI-generated content sometimes needs editing for accuracy.

Slice: The Customizable Powerhouse

Slice (formerly Slite) has evolved into a presentation tool with unprecedented customization. It's less about AI generation and more about collaborative, beautiful presentation building.

Slice strengths:

  • Design Flexibility: Every element is customizable. Total control over layout, colors, fonts.
  • Brand Control: Deep brand kit system ensuring complete consistency.
  • Team Collaboration: Comments, version history, approval workflows.
  • Content Library: Integrated asset library with brand-approved images and graphics.
  • Export Quality: PDFs and videos export beautifully.
  • Interactive Elements: Add polls, quizzes, and interactive content to presentations.

Slice's weakness: less AI-powered generation than Pitch. You build more manually, which takes longer. Steeper learning curve. Also more expensive at $20+/month.

Real-World Test: Startup Pitch Deck

With Pitch: "Create a Series A pitch deck for a SaaS platform" → 20 slides generated with content and design in 10 minutes. Quality: Good. Feedback from investors: "Solid deck, content could be stronger." Time to presentation-ready: 2 hours (including content editing).

With Slice: Started with blank presentation, designed manually using brand kit, added custom graphics, built interactive final slide. Quality: Excellent, very polished. Time: 6 hours. Feedback: "This deck is beautifully crafted."

Winner depends on deadline. Pitch if you have hours, Slice if you have days.

AI Capabilities

Pitch AI: Content generation and slide deck creation. Tell it what you want, it builds it. Good for rapid iteration.

Slice AI: More limited. Primarily design suggestions and layout recommendations. Not content generation focused.

Pricing & Value

Pitch: Free tier useful for trying it out. Pro at $13/month for individuals, $50/month for teams.

Slice: No free tier. Starts at $20/month for individuals. Enterprise pricing available.

For startup founders: Pitch is likely better ROI. For corporate teams: Slice's control and collaboration features justify the cost.

Which Should You Use?

Use Pitch if:

  • You need presentations fast (hours, not days)
  • You want AI to generate content and layout
  • You're a founder or individual contributor
  • Budget is a concern

Use Slice if:

  • You need pixel-perfect design control
  • Your brand consistency is critical
  • You're a design team or corporate marketing
  • You need approval workflows and version control

Honest take: Most people should start with Pitch. It's faster, cheaper, and handles 80% of presentation needs. Only move to Slice if you outgrow Pitch's customization.
