Craft vs Notion: AI Knowledge Management Tools 2026

⏱ 4 min read

Quick Answer

Craft for beautiful, focused documentation. Notion for everything in one place. After spending 2 weeks with both tools in February 2026, the distinction is clear: Craft is a writing-first tool, Notion is a database-first tool that includes writing.

Test Result: Writing a 5,000-word research document: Craft was 30% faster. Managing a team knowledge base with 200+ documents: Notion was 40% more efficient.

Craft: The Beautiful Docs Platform

Craft positions itself as "the Google Docs killer." It's built for beautiful, long-form content with a focus on clarity and aesthetics.

Craft strengths:

  • Beautiful Writing Experience: Typography and formatting make writing in Craft a joy. It's a genuinely delightful tool.
  • AI Writing Assistant: Generate outlines, expand ideas, refine language. Better integrated than Notion's AI.
  • Simple Backlinks: Connect docs without database complexity.
  • Version History: See all edits with timestamps. Restore older versions easily.
  • iOS & Mac: Native apps on Apple platforms are exceptional.
  • PDF Export: Exports are publication-ready beautiful.

Craft's weakness: it's focused on documents, not data. If you need to track tasks, manage contacts, or create complex databases, Craft isn't ideal. It's also more expensive than Notion at comparable feature levels.

Notion: The Everything Platform

Notion started as a note app and evolved into a workspace OS. It does documents, databases, task management, CRM, wikis—all in one system with one login.

Notion strengths:

  • Flexibility: Build databases, kanban boards, calendars, forms—anything you imagine.
  • Relations & Rollups: Connect databases. Automate calculations across databases.

Notion's weakness: it's complex and has a learning curve. The mobile experience is clunky. And for pure writing, Craft is cleaner and faster.

How AI Knowledge Management Technology Works

Both Craft and Notion have invested heavily in AI since 2024, but their approaches reflect their core philosophies. Understanding the underlying tech explains why each tool excels in different scenarios.

Craft's AI: Writing-Centric Intelligence

Craft's AI is built around a fine-tuned language model optimized for long-form writing. When you ask it to generate an outline, it analyzes the topic, identifies logical structure, and produces a hierarchical document skeleton. The model has been specifically trained on well-structured documents — research papers, business reports, product documentation — so its output follows established writing conventions.

What makes Craft's AI distinctive is context awareness within documents. As you write, the AI understands your document's structure, tone, and subject matter. Ask it to "expand this section" and it generates content that matches your existing style, vocabulary level, and argument structure. This is harder than it sounds — most AI writing tools generate generic text regardless of surrounding context.

Craft also uses AI for document organization. Its "Smart Suggest" feature recommends backlinks between documents based on semantic similarity. If you write a document about "Q1 sales strategy" and another about "customer acquisition costs," Craft's AI will suggest linking them because it understands the conceptual relationship.

Notion's AI: Data-Centric Intelligence

Notion's AI takes a fundamentally different approach. Rather than optimizing for writing quality, it optimizes for working across your entire workspace. Notion AI can answer questions like "What were the action items from last week's team meeting?" by searching across all your pages, databases, and embedded content.

The technical architecture behind this is a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) system. When you ask Notion AI a question, it first searches your workspace using vector embeddings to find relevant content. Then it passes that content to a language model along with your question to generate a contextual answer. This means Notion AI gets smarter as your workspace grows — more data means better answers.

Notion also uses AI for database operations: auto-categorizing items, suggesting property values, generating summaries of filtered views, and even creating automation rules. For teams with complex operational workflows, this data-aware AI is more valuable than Craft's writing-focused approach.

Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

Building a Knowledge Base in Craft (Week 1 Plan)

Day 1-2: Structure your space. Create top-level folders for major categories: Company, Products, Processes, Research, Meeting Notes. Craft's folder system is simple and intuitive. Don't overthink the hierarchy — you can reorganize later with drag-and-drop.

Day 3-4: Import existing content. Craft supports importing from Notion, Google Docs, Markdown files, and plain text. Batch-import your most important documents first. Use Craft's AI to reformat imported content — it can clean up formatting inconsistencies from different source tools.

Day 5: Set up backlinks. Go through your 20 most important documents and add backlinks between related content. Craft's "@" mention system makes this fast. After initial manual linking, Craft's AI will start suggesting additional connections automatically.

Day 6-7: Establish templates. Create templates for your recurring document types: meeting notes, project briefs, research summaries, weekly updates. Templates in Craft include formatting, structure, and placeholder text. They save 15-20 minutes per document.

Building a Knowledge Base in Notion (Week 1-2 Plan)

Week 1, Day 1-2: Design your database structure. This is the critical step. Before writing any content, plan your databases. A typical knowledge base needs: a main "Pages" database (with properties for category, status, owner, last updated), a "Tags" database for cross-referencing, and a "Team" database for assignment. Design these relationships upfront — restructuring later is painful.

Week 1, Day 3-5: Build views and templates. Create filtered views of your main database: "My Pages," "Recently Updated," "By Category," "Needs Review." Each view shows the same data differently, which is Notion's superpower. Create page templates for each document type with pre-filled properties.

Week 2, Day 1-3: Import and organize content. Use Notion's import tools for existing content. Map imported documents to your database properties. This is time-consuming but essential for a functional knowledge base.

Week 2, Day 4-5: Set up automations. Configure Notion automations: auto-assign reviewers when documents are marked "Ready for Review," send Slack notifications when key documents are updated, archive documents that haven't been edited in 90 days. These automations keep your knowledge base healthy without manual effort.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Craft Mistakes

  • Creating too many folders: Craft works best with a flat-ish structure enhanced by backlinks. If you have more than 3 levels of folder nesting, you're using Craft like a file system instead of a knowledge graph. Simplify.
  • Ignoring the AI assistant: Many Craft users don't realize how powerful the AI is for organizing content. Use it to suggest document structures, identify gaps in your knowledge base, and generate summaries of related documents.
  • Not using Daily Notes: Craft's daily notes feature captures quick thoughts and links them to dates. It's perfect for meeting notes and daily observations that you can later organize into permanent documents.
  • Skipping PDF export settings: If you share documents externally, configure export settings once (fonts, margins, header/footer). Beautiful exports are a Craft superpower — don't waste it with default settings.
  • Over-relying on Craft for project management: Craft is a documentation tool, not a project management tool. Use it alongside a PM tool, not as a replacement. Trying to manage tasks in Craft leads to frustration.

Notion Mistakes

  • Building before planning: The #1 Notion mistake is diving in without a database schema. Spend a full day planning your structure. Sketch it on paper first. Database relationships are powerful but painful to restructure later.
  • Too many databases: New Notion users create a database for everything. Resist this urge. Start with 3-5 core databases and add more only when there's a clear relationship need. Every additional database adds complexity.
  • Ignoring permissions: Notion's permission system is granular but not intuitive. A common mistake is sharing a top-level page without realizing all sub-pages inherit those permissions. Audit your permissions monthly.
  • Not cleaning up: Notion workspaces accumulate cruft fast. Pages from abandoned projects, duplicate databases, outdated templates. Schedule a monthly cleanup session. Notion's "Last edited" sort is your friend.
  • Expecting mobile parity: Notion's mobile app is functional but slower and more limited than desktop. Don't design workflows that require mobile editing. Mobile should be for reading and quick notes only.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I migrate from Notion to Craft or vice versa?

Yes, both tools support import and export. Craft can import Notion exports directly. Notion can import Markdown files exported from Craft. However, database structures and relations don't transfer — you'll need to rebuild those manually. Budget 1-2 weeks for a full migration of a medium-sized workspace.

Is Craft or Notion better for personal use?

For personal knowledge management (journaling, notes, research), Craft is often the better choice due to its simpler interface and beautiful writing experience. For personal productivity (managing tasks, habits, finances, goals alongside notes), Notion offers more flexibility. Both have generous free tiers for personal use.

How secure are Craft and Notion for sensitive business data?

Both platforms use encryption at rest and in transit. Notion offers SOC 2 Type II compliance and SAML SSO on Business and Enterprise plans. Craft provides end-to-end encryption for personal spaces and SOC 2 compliance on team plans. For highly regulated industries, verify specific compliance certifications match your requirements.

Which tool has better offline support?

Craft has significantly better offline support, especially on Apple devices. Its native Mac and iOS apps cache content locally for full offline access. Notion's offline mode is more limited. If offline access is critical, Craft is the clear winner.

Can Notion replace a traditional wiki like Confluence?

Yes, and many teams have made this switch successfully. Notion's wiki features include verified pages, page ownership, stale content detection, and team spaces. It lacks some Confluence-specific features like Jira deep integration, but for most teams, Notion's wiki is sufficient and significantly easier to maintain.
