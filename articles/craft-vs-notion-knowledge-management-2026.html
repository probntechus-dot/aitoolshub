Craft vs Notion: AI Knowledge Management Tools 2026

By Sarah Mitchell | March 2, 2026 | 8 min read

Quick Answer

Craft for beautiful, focused documentation. Notion for everything in one place. After spending 2 weeks with both tools in February 2026, the distinction is clear: Craft is a writing-first tool, Notion is a database-first tool that includes writing.

Test Result: Writing a 5,000-word research document: Craft was 30% faster. Managing a team knowledge base with 200+ documents: Notion was 40% more efficient.

Craft: The Beautiful Docs Platform

Craft positions itself as "the Google Docs killer." It's built for beautiful, long-form content with a focus on clarity and aesthetics.

Craft strengths:

  • Beautiful Writing Experience: Typography and formatting make writing in Craft a joy. It's a genuinely delightful tool.
  • AI Writing Assistant: Generate outlines, expand ideas, refine language. Better integrated than Notion's AI.
  • Simple Backlinks: Connect docs without database complexity.
  • Version History: See all edits with timestamps. Restore older versions easily.
  • iOS & Mac: Native apps on Apple platforms are exceptional.
  • PDF Export: Exports are publication-ready beautiful.

Craft's weakness: it's focused on documents, not data. If you need to track tasks, manage contacts, or create complex databases, Craft isn't ideal. It's also more expensive than Notion at comparable feature levels.

Pricing: Free tier available, Craft Pro is $10/month.

Notion: The Everything Platform

Notion started as a note app and evolved into a workspace OS. It does documents, databases, task management, CRM, wikis—all in one system with one login.

Notion strengths:

  • Flexibility: Build databases, kanban boards, calendars, forms—anything you imagine.
  • Relations & Rollups: Connect databases. Automate calculations across databases.
  • Template Gallery: 1000s of ready-made templates for every use case.
  • Integrations: Connect to Slack, Gmail, Zapier, 100+ apps.
  • Collaboration: Real-time collaboration with detailed permissions.
  • AI Features: Notion AI generates content, summarizes, and answers questions about your workspace.
  • All-in-One: Replace 5+ tools with Notion.

Notion's weakness: it's complex and has a learning curve. The mobile experience is clunky. And for pure writing, Craft is cleaner and faster.

Pricing: Free tier available, Plus ($10/month/user), Business ($18/month/user).

Real Scenarios

Scenario 1: Solo writer/researcher building a knowledge base

Craft. You'll love the writing experience, and simple backlinks are sufficient for discovery. Cost: $10/month.

Scenario 2: Team knowledge base with 20+ documents and related data

Notion. You need database relationships and team collaboration. Cost: $10-18/month per user.

Scenario 3: Project management with documentation

Notion. Build a database of projects, link to docs, manage tasks—all integrated.

Scenario 4: Publishing research or whitepapers

Craft. The export quality and visual aesthetics are superior.

The AI Comparison

Craft AI: Dedicated writing assistant. Generates outlines, expands sections, refines language. Good at maintaining your voice.

Notion AI: General-purpose. Summarizes database contents, generates content, answers questions about your workspace. Better integrated with your data but less specialized for writing.

My Honest Take

In 2026, the question isn't which is objectively better—it's which matches your workflow. Use Craft if you write a lot and want a beautiful experience. Use Notion if you need flexibility and want everything in one place.

Many teams do both: Craft for writing/publishing, Notion for operations/databases. Cost: $20-30/month per person. Reasonable for productivity.
