AI eDiscovery and Litigation Support Tools in 2026

By Sarah Mitchell March 10, 2026 15 min read

Document review has always been the cost black hole of litigation. A mid-sized employment dispute might involve 500,000 documents. A major commercial litigation can reach 10 million. At $1.50 per document for first-pass manual review, the math gets ugly fast.

That's why I've spent the past three months stress-testing six AI eDiscovery platforms with a real 250,000-document dataset from a completed commercial litigation matter. I wanted to answer two questions that every litigator and litigation support professional is asking: Which AI tools actually reduce review costs without sacrificing accuracy? And is generative AI ready for the defensibility standards that courts require?

The answers surprised me. Generative AI in eDiscovery has leaped from experimental to production-ready faster than almost anyone expected — but the platforms differ dramatically in how they implement it and what it costs.

The Test Methodology

I used a 250,000-document dataset from a completed breach-of-contract matter where the full manual review results were known. This gave me a ground truth to measure against. For each platform, I tested:

  • Processing speed: Time from data upload to reviewable documents
  • AI classification accuracy: Precision and recall against the known relevant document set
  • Privilege detection: How well the AI identified potentially privileged communications
  • User experience: How quickly a new reviewer could become productive
  • Cost: Total platform cost for the 250K document set over a 6-month matter lifecycle

1. RelativityOne with aiR — Most Powerful AI Suite

RelativityOne has been the dominant eDiscovery platform for years, and their aiR suite (built in collaboration with Microsoft using Azure OpenAI) cements that position. The generative AI capabilities represent the most significant upgrade to the platform since the move to cloud.

aiR for Review doesn't just classify documents as relevant or not — it reads each document and provides a written explanation of its relevance determination. On my test dataset, it achieved 91% precision and 87% recall on the first pass, which is genuinely remarkable. More importantly, the explanations were cogent enough that reviewers could quickly confirm or override the AI's decision without reading the entire document.

aiR for Privilege was the standout feature. Privilege review is the most expensive and highest-risk phase of document review — miss one privileged document in a production and you've got a serious problem. aiR for Privilege identified 94% of the documents that manual reviewers coded as privileged, and its explanations correctly identified the attorney-client relationship, the communication's confidential nature, and the legal advice being sought. It even caught three privileged documents that the manual review team had miscoded as non-privileged.

The new unlimited fixed-fee pricing per GB is a game-changer for cost predictability. Previously, generative AI features carried per-document charges that made large-scale deployment economically questionable. The fixed per-GB model means you can run aiR across your entire document population without worrying about a surprise bill.

Pricing: RelativityOne uses per-GB pricing for hosting with separate licensing for features. The new unlimited aiR pricing is available through both transactional and subscription models. Partners like Cimplifi and IntrepidX have introduced all-inclusive per-GB rates that bundle aiR capabilities. Expect total costs of $25–$50+ per GB per month for fully featured deployments, varying by volume commitments.

Best for: Large, complex matters with high document volumes where privilege risk is significant and defensibility is paramount.

2. Everlaw — Best User Experience and Collaboration

Everlaw has always been the platform that lawyers actually enjoy using (a low bar, perhaps, but genuine). Their 2026 AI enhancements maintain that philosophy — powerful capabilities wrapped in an interface that doesn't require a litigation support specialist to navigate.

Everlaw's AI-powered predictive coding reached 88% precision and 85% recall on my test dataset — slightly below RelativityOne but still very strong. Where Everlaw excelled was in the collaborative workflow. Multiple reviewers could work simultaneously with real-time AI model updates, meaning the predictive coding got smarter as reviewers worked through documents. The model converged to stable results faster than any other platform I tested.

The narrative building and trial preparation tools are unique to Everlaw. After the review was complete, I used the platform to build a case narrative — it organized key documents into a chronological storyline, identified contradictions between witness testimony and contemporaneous documents, and generated a timeline visualization that would be immediately useful in trial preparation.

Early Case Assessment (ECA) deserves special mention. Before committing to a full review, Everlaw's ECA module processed my dataset and within hours provided a statistical estimate of relevant document volume, key custodian analysis, and date distribution. This information alone — which would have taken a team days to compile manually — can save six figures in discovery costs by informing negotiations over search terms and custodian scope.

Pricing: Everlaw uses transparent per-GB annual pricing. Plans range from ECA-only to full platform access with predictive coding and trial preparation. Specific per-GB rates are available on their pricing page and vary based on annual commitment. Industry estimates suggest $20–$40 per GB per month for standard deployments.

Best for: Mid-market and large litigation firms that prioritize user experience, collaboration, and trial preparation alongside document review.

3. DISCO — Best Transparent Pricing and Speed

DISCO's value proposition has always been simplicity: a flat per-GB rate that includes everything — processing, hosting, analytics, AI, production, unlimited users. No surprise line items. No "oh, that feature is extra" conversations with your sales rep.

Their AI assistant, DISCO Cecilia (powered by generative AI), represents a significant capability leap. I asked Cecilia to summarize the key themes in my document population, and within minutes it produced a remarkably coherent overview of the five primary dispute themes, the key date ranges for each, and the custodians most central to each theme. That analysis would have taken a review team several days of first-pass review to develop.

Processing speed was where DISCO genuinely outperformed. My 250K document dataset was processed and reviewable in under 4 hours. RelativityOne took about 6 hours, and Everlaw about 8 hours for the same dataset. In matters where time is critical — emergency TRO motions, regulatory investigations with tight deadlines — that speed difference matters.

On classification accuracy, DISCO's predictive coding achieved 86% precision and 83% recall. Solid, but a notch below Everlaw and RelativityOne. The trade-off is the pricing clarity and processing speed.

Pricing: DISCO charges a flat per-GB fee that includes all features — processing, loading, OCR, analytics, AI, users, production, and predictive coding. No à la carte charges. This is a genuine differentiator in an industry notorious for billing complexity. DISCO also offers a free tier for smaller matters. Typical per-GB rates are competitive, and the all-inclusive model means your actual cost matches your quote.

Best for: Firms that want predictable costs, fast processing, and refuse to deal with complex eDiscovery billing models.

4. Reveal — Best for AI-First Review Workflows

Reveal has positioned itself as the most AI-forward eDiscovery platform, and it shows in their workflow design. While other platforms bolt AI onto existing review interfaces, Reveal was rebuilt around AI-first workflows where human review is targeted at the documents that need it most.

Their BRAINSPACE analytics engine has been a respected tool for years, and the 2026 generative AI integration deepens its capabilities. The concept clustering is notably sophisticated — rather than simple keyword grouping, Reveal identifies conceptual relationships across documents using neural embeddings. On my test dataset, it identified a cluster of documents related to a side agreement between executives that wasn't captured by any of the agreed-upon search terms. In a real matter, that cluster could have been the smoking gun.

The continuous active learning (CAL) implementation was also strong. I started with just 50 seed documents coded by a senior reviewer, and Reveal's AI progressively identified the remaining relevant documents with 85% precision and 88% recall — notably high recall, which means fewer relevant documents slipping through the cracks.

Pricing: Reveal offers per-GB pricing with options for full-service managed review or self-service platform access. They emphasize predictable rate structures. Pricing details are available through their sales team, with industry estimates suggesting competitive per-GB rates for the feature set.

Best for: Firms running high-volume reviews that want to minimize human review hours while maintaining high recall rates.

5. Logikcull (by Reveal) — Best for In-House Legal Teams

Logikcull, now part of the Reveal family, maintains its position as the most accessible eDiscovery platform for in-house legal teams that don't have dedicated litigation support staff. The interface is designed for lawyers, not technologists.

I tested Logikcull with a simulated internal investigation scenario — a data breach response requiring rapid review of 50,000 employee communications. The platform's strength was speed-to-review. Data was uploaded, processed, and searchable within 2 hours. The AI-powered culling reduced the reviewable set by 65% based on relevance predictions, and the remaining documents were organized into intuitive clusters that non-specialist reviewers could navigate efficiently.

The self-service model is the key differentiator. I set up the entire matter, loaded data, configured analytics, and began review without contacting a support team or project manager. For in-house teams that need to respond quickly to regulatory inquiries or internal investigations, that autonomy is invaluable.

Pricing: Logikcull uses straightforward per-GB pricing with no per-user charges — every lawyer in your organization can access the platform. Plans are available on a pay-as-you-go basis, making it ideal for teams with variable eDiscovery needs.

Best for: Corporate legal departments handling internal investigations, regulatory responses, and routine litigation without dedicated litigation support staff.

6. eDiscovery AI — Best Specialized AI Add-On

eDiscovery AI takes a different approach than the full-platform providers. Rather than replacing your existing eDiscovery platform, it integrates with them — adding advanced AI classification capabilities to RelativityOne, Everlaw, or other hosting environments.

The value proposition is clear for teams already invested in a platform but wanting stronger AI. Their classification models are trained on legal domain-specific data and can be fine-tuned for specific matter types. I tested their model on my breach-of-contract dataset, and it achieved 89% precision — comparable to native platform AI — but with the advantage of being platform-agnostic.

Pricing: eDiscovery AI charges per-document processing fees, making it economical for targeted AI deployment on specific document sets within larger matters. Custom pricing based on volume.

Best for: Teams with existing eDiscovery platform investments that want to layer on additional AI classification power.

The Defensibility Question

Every litigator asks this: "If I use AI for document review, can opposing counsel challenge it?"

The short answer: technology-assisted review has been defensible since Judge Andrew Peck's landmark 2012 ruling in Rio Tinto PLC v. Vale S.A., and subsequent decisions have reinforced its acceptance. Courts increasingly view AI-assisted review as more defensible than purely manual review because it offers statistical verifiability — you can demonstrate recall rates and confidence intervals in a way that manual review simply cannot.

The key to defensibility in 2026 is documentation. Whatever platform you use, maintain records of:

  • The AI model training methodology (seed set selection, iterative training rounds)
  • Validation sampling results (precision, recall, F1 scores)
  • Human quality control measures (senior reviewer oversight, random sampling)
  • The decision not to review documents below the AI's relevance threshold, with statistical justification

  • Everlaw: Transparent per-GB annual pricing makes budgeting predictable. Mid-range cost for the feature set.
  • DISCO: The most predictable cost — flat per-GB, no surprises. Total cost was the easiest to quote accurately upfront.
  • Reveal: Competitive per-GB rates with the strongest AI-first workflow.
  • Logikcull: The lowest cost option for the self-service model, ideal for smaller matters.

My Final Recommendation

Choosing an eDiscovery platform is a bigger decision than choosing most legal tech tools because migration costs are real and matter data gets locked in. Here's how I'd frame the decision:

  • If you handle large, complex matters: RelativityOne with aiR offers the most powerful AI capabilities, especially for privilege detection. The new fixed-fee pricing makes it economically viable to deploy AI at scale.
  • If you value user experience and collaboration: Everlaw's interface and trial preparation tools are best-in-class. Your lawyers will actually use it without extensive training.
  • If pricing predictability is your top priority: DISCO's all-inclusive flat per-GB model eliminates billing surprises and includes their Cecilia AI.
  • If you're an in-house team: Logikcull's self-service model gives you autonomy without depending on outside vendors for every investigation.

The broader takeaway: AI in eDiscovery isn't optional in 2026. If your opposing counsel is using AI to find relevant documents in days while your team is still doing first-pass manual review in weeks, you're not just spending more — you're potentially missing key evidence. The technology is proven, the courts accept it, and the cost savings are real.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does AI eDiscovery software cost per GB?

AI eDiscovery pricing varies by platform. DISCO uses a flat per-GB model including all features. RelativityOne offers unlimited fixed-fee per-GB pricing for aiR. Everlaw uses per-GB annual pricing. Typical costs range from $15–$75 per GB per month, with enterprise agreements potentially lower.

What is the best AI eDiscovery platform in 2026?

RelativityOne with aiR is the most powerful for large, complex matters. Everlaw offers the best user experience. DISCO provides the most transparent pricing. The best choice depends on your firm's size, matter complexity, and pricing preferences.

Can AI replace human document reviewers in eDiscovery?

AI reduces the volume of documents requiring human review by 60–80%, but human review remains essential for privilege determinations, hot document identification, and quality control. Courts accept AI-assisted review but require defensible methodology documentation.

What is Relativity aiR and how does it work?

Relativity aiR is RelativityOne's generative AI suite built with Microsoft Azure OpenAI. It includes aiR for Review (relevance with explanations), aiR for Privilege (privilege identification), and generative features for summarization and narrative building — all within RelativityOne's secure cloud.

Is AI-assisted eDiscovery review defensible in court?

Yes. Courts have accepted technology-assisted review since Judge Peck's Rio Tinto decision in 2012. AI-assisted review is now widely considered more defensible than manual review due to its consistency and statistical verifiability. The key is transparent methodology documentation and validation sampling.
