I've been a Notion user for two years. When they launched the Notion AI add-on, I was both excited and skeptical. Excited because I already lived in Notion. Skeptical because the monthly add-on cost on top of an existing subscription felt like a cash grab.

After 60 days of using Notion AI for everything from project summaries to client documents, here's the honest verdict: it's worth it, but not for the reasons Notion's marketing suggests.

What Notion AI Actually Does Well

Meeting Summaries Are Genuinely Excellent

Copy your meeting transcript (from Otter.ai, for instance) into a Notion page, ask AI to summarize it with action items ‚Äî and you get a polished summary in seconds. I've stopped manually writing meeting notes entirely. This feature alone is probably worth $16/month for anyone who has more than 5 meetings per week.

Document First Drafts

Give Notion AI a bullet-point outline and it generates a full document draft. Useful for internal documents, client-facing summaries, and project briefs. Quality is solid ‚Äî not spectacular, but saves significant time on first drafts.

Table Filling & Data Extraction

One genuinely unique feature: select a bunch of text, ask AI to fill a table from it. Useful for extracting structured data from unstructured notes. I used this to turn 200 lines of client interview notes into a structured comparison table. Took 30 seconds instead of 45 minutes.

Where Notion AI Falls Short

The AI doesn't know your workspace context. It can't pull information from other pages to inform its responses. Each AI interaction is essentially stateless. This limits how smart it can be ‚Äî you're paying for a text editor AI, not a workspace assistant.

Compared to ChatGPT with a similar prompt, the output quality is slightly lower. The convenience of working within Notion offsets this, but if you're not already a Notion user, there's no reason to adopt it just for the AI features.

Notion AI review verdict: good, not great. If you're deep in the Notion ecosystem with a team, it's a reasonable $10/month that saves meaningful time. If you're evaluating whether to adopt Notion specifically for AI features ‚Äî look elsewhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Notion AI worth the monthly cost?

Notion AI is worth it if you're already a heavy Notion user who generates many documents and meeting notes. For teams with 5+ weekly meetings, the meeting summary feature alone saves enough time to justify the cost. It's not worth it if you primarily use Notion as a personal notes app or if you already have ChatGPT Plus (which offers more capable AI for less per task).

What can Notion AI actually do?

Notion AI can write and edit text within pages, generate first drafts from bullet outlines, summarize long documents and meeting transcripts, extract structured data into tables, answer questions about content on a page, translate text, and fix spelling and grammar. It cannot pull context from other Notion pages or databases ‚Äî each interaction is stateless.

How does Notion AI compare to ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is generally more capable than Notion AI for most writing tasks. Notion AI's advantage is integration ‚Äî it works directly in your Notion workspace without switching tabs. If you already have ChatGPT Plus, Notion AI provides modest additional value. If you're choosing between them, ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) offers more flexibility across more tasks than Notion AI ($10/month add-on).

Can Notion AI access information from other pages in my workspace?

No ‚Äî Notion AI cannot cross-reference other pages in your workspace. It only works with the content on the current page you're using. This is a significant limitation compared to tools like Guru or Notion Q&A (a separate feature). Each AI interaction is essentially independent, which limits how smart the AI can be about your overall business context.

Who should use Notion AI?

Notion AI is best for: small teams (5‚Äì20 people) already using Notion for project management, businesses with high meeting and documentation volume, and teams who want AI writing assistance without switching between apps. It's not ideal for solo users, businesses not already using Notion, or anyone needing AI to reason across multiple documents simultaneously.
