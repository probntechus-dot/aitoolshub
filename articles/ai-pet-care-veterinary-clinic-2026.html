AI Tools for Pet Care Businesses and Veterinary Clinics

By Sarah Mitchell March 15, 2026 9 min read

The first time I saw an AI system detect a potential cardiac abnormality in an X-ray before the veterinarian reviewed it, I was genuinely stunned. It was during a visit to a progressive multi-doctor veterinary clinic that had adopted AI diagnostic imaging tools. The radiologist on call confirmed the finding minutes later. "The AI doesn't replace our judgment," the veterinarian told me, "but it means nothing gets missed at 5 p.m. on a Friday when we're all tired."

The pet care industry — comprising veterinary medicine, grooming, boarding, training, and pet sitting — has been slower to adopt technology than many other service sectors. That's rapidly changing. Americans spend over $140 billion annually on their pets, and competition for pet owner loyalty is fierce. The practices and businesses adopting AI are building advantages that will be difficult for competitors to overcome.

I've spent months evaluating AI tools across every segment of the pet care industry. Here's what the best-in-class looks like today.

AI Diagnostic Tools for Veterinary Clinics

The most transformative AI applications in veterinary medicine are in diagnostics — tools that help veterinarians catch conditions they might otherwise miss, faster and with greater confidence.

Aidoc and similar AI radiology platforms are now being adopted by forward-thinking veterinary hospitals to analyze digital radiographs and flag potential abnormalities for prioritized review. While originally developed for human medicine, AI radiology tools have been validated for veterinary applications — particularly for orthopedic and thoracic imaging.

SignalPET is purpose-built for veterinary radiology AI. It analyzes digital radiographs and provides instant preliminary reads with annotated findings — particularly useful for general practice veterinarians who see radiography results without an on-site specialist. Starting at around $149/month, SignalPET gives practices access to AI-assisted interpretation that previously required consulting with a specialist radiologist at significant cost and delay.

Vetology AI offers similar AI radiograph interpretation specifically optimized for companion animal species. For clinics that regularly perform imaging but don't have radiologist access, these tools serve as an important safety net while reducing time to diagnosis.

Heska's Element series and other point-of-care diagnostic equipment now incorporate AI in analyzers that interpret blood and urine results, flagging abnormal values and identifying patterns associated with specific conditions. This kind of AI-assisted interpretation at the point of care speeds clinical decision-making significantly.

AI-Powered Practice Management Software for Veterinary Clinics

Running a veterinary clinic involves an enormous amount of administrative complexity — appointment scheduling, medical records, inventory management, client communication, billing, and regulatory compliance. AI-enhanced practice management software is addressing all of it.

Covetrus Pulse (formerly AVImark) is one of the most widely used veterinary practice management systems, with AI features including automated appointment reminders, wellness plan compliance tracking, and inventory reorder automation. Its AI can identify patients overdue for preventive care and automatically generate reminder campaigns — a major driver of recall revenue that many clinics leave on the table.

Shepherd Veterinary Software is a newer cloud-native platform built from the ground up with AI in mind. Its AI-assisted SOAP note generation helps veterinarians document appointments faster — they can dictate or type notes conversationally and the system structures them into proper medical records format automatically. For busy practices where documentation time is a significant burden, this is a substantial time saver. Pricing starts at ~$250/month.

eVetPractice (by Covetrus) offers strong cloud-based practice management with AI-powered inventory management that tracks controlled substance usage and automatically generates DEA compliance reports. For practices managing Schedule II–V medications, this compliance automation is invaluable.

Vetspire is gaining traction as a modern, AI-enhanced veterinary EHR with strong client communication features. Its AI clinical decision support suggests treatment protocols and drug dosages based on the patient's signalment and presenting complaint — serving as a helpful reference that accelerates clinical decision-making without replacing veterinary judgment.

AI Telehealth and Remote Triage for Pet Care

Pet telehealth exploded during the COVID-19 pandemic and has become a permanent fixture in veterinary care delivery. AI is making remote assessment more sophisticated and accessible.

Vetster is one of the leading veterinary telehealth platforms, connecting pet owners with licensed veterinarians for video consultations. Increasingly, AI-powered pre-visit assessments help triage client concerns — pets with emergent symptoms are directed to in-person emergency care, while non-urgent issues are routed to telehealth consultations. This AI triage function reduces inappropriate telehealth consultations and ensures emergency cases aren't delayed.

Fuzzy Pet Health offers an AI-enhanced pet health membership that includes 24/7 text chat with veterinary professionals, AI-powered symptom assessment, and an extensive pet health knowledge base. For subscription-based pet health models emerging in 2025–2026, AI triage is the backbone that makes the economics work.

PetDesk includes AI-powered client communication tools that can answer common pet health questions via chat, reducing call volume to the clinic while keeping clients engaged and informed. Practices using PetDesk report significant reductions in routine phone inquiries — freeing reception staff for higher-value interactions. Starting at around $199/month.

AI Tools for Pet Grooming Businesses

Pet grooming is a high-volume, appointment-driven service business where scheduling efficiency, client retention, and staff productivity directly impact profitability. AI tools designed for the grooming sector are delivering measurable results.

MoeGo has emerged as the dominant AI-powered grooming management platform. Starting at $25/month, MoeGo includes AI-powered appointment optimization that builds schedules based on pet characteristics (size, breed, coat condition), groomer specializations, and appointment history. Its smart routing feature optimizes the sequence of appointments to minimize downtime. MoeGo's automated client communication sends customized reminders, follow-ups, and rebooking prompts that have been shown to reduce no-shows by up to 40%.

Groomers Helper focuses specifically on the workflow optimization side, with AI that learns individual groomer preferences and speeds over time to create more accurate time estimates. For grooming shops that have struggled with appointments running over and cascading schedule delays, this kind of intelligent time estimation is a game-changer.

Gingr is a broader pet business management platform covering grooming, boarding, and training that includes AI reporting and analytics. Its AI-generated business insights surface which services are most profitable, which clients are most loyal, and where scheduling inefficiencies are costing money. Starting at $99/month, Gingr is well-suited for multi-service pet businesses.

AI for Pet Boarding and Daycare Facilities

Pet boarding and daycare operations face unique AI opportunities — from occupancy optimization to pet behavior monitoring to client communication.

Kennel Connection and Revelation Pets both offer AI-enhanced boarding management with automated waitlist management, occupancy forecasting, and client communication workflows. For facilities managing dozens or hundreds of pets simultaneously, AI-powered capacity planning prevents both under-booking (lost revenue) and overbooking (quality and safety risks).

Dogtopia's proprietary AI webcam monitoring system (used across its franchise network) uses computer vision to monitor dog play areas and flag potential conflict situations before they escalate. Staff receive real-time alerts when the AI detects dog body language associated with escalating tension — reducing bite incidents and creating a demonstrably safer environment that facilities use as a marketing differentiator.

Computer vision applications for monitoring animal behavior in group settings are expanding. Startups in this space are developing AI systems that track individual animal behavior patterns over time, flagging changes that might indicate health issues or stress — providing a layer of monitoring that human staff simply can't maintain across large groups.

AI for Pet Business Marketing and Client Retention

Acquiring pet owners is expensive; keeping them is the key to sustainable pet business economics. AI is dramatically improving how pet businesses communicate with clients and prevent churn.

PetDesk and Weave (starting at ~$400/month for the full suite) both offer AI-powered client communication platforms specifically designed for veterinary practices. They manage appointment reminders, recall campaigns, post-visit follow-ups, and review generation automatically — workflows that are proven revenue drivers but are nearly impossible to execute consistently without automation.

Veterinary practices using automated recall campaigns consistently see 15–25% more preventive care appointments than practices managing outreach manually. At a typical preventive exam revenue of $200–$400, this translates directly to meaningful revenue recovery.

Broadly is an AI-powered reputation management tool used by many pet care businesses to automatically request reviews from satisfied clients and manage responses. For businesses where online reviews are a primary driver of new client acquisition, maintaining a high Google rating is worth significant investment — and Broadly's automation makes it nearly effortless.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are AI diagnostic tools for veterinary clinics accurate enough to trust?

AI diagnostic tools like SignalPET and Vetology are designed as decision support aids, not replacements for veterinary judgment. Studies on AI radiograph analysis in veterinary medicine have shown sensitivity and specificity comparable to or exceeding general practitioners for certain conditions — particularly orthopedic and cardiac findings. The most honest framing is that AI reduces the chance of something being missed, especially in high-volume or high-fatigue situations, while final diagnostic authority always rests with the veterinarian.

What is the best booking software for a pet grooming business?

MoeGo is widely regarded as the best AI-powered scheduling platform specifically designed for pet grooming businesses. Its AI appointment optimization accounts for pet characteristics, groomer specializations, and historical appointment data to build efficient schedules and minimize gaps. For grooming shops embedded within a larger pet care facility offering boarding and daycare, Gingr offers more comprehensive multi-service management at a slightly higher price point.

How can AI help a veterinary clinic increase preventive care revenue?

AI-powered client communication tools like PetDesk and Weave automatically identify patients overdue for preventive care (annual exams, vaccines, dental cleanings, heartworm tests) and send personalized outreach to their owners. These automated recall campaigns require minimal staff time but consistently recover 15–25% more preventive visits compared to manual or ad-hoc outreach. At typical preventive care revenue of $200–$400+ per visit, even a small improvement in recall rates generates significant annual revenue.

Is pet telehealth with AI triage safe for pets with serious symptoms?

Reputable AI triage systems in pet telehealth are explicitly designed to identify potentially emergent symptoms and direct pet owners to in-person emergency care. They're calibrated to err on the side of recommending in-person evaluation when symptoms could indicate serious conditions. That said, pet telehealth and AI triage are best used for non-urgent consultations, follow-up questions, and behavior or nutrition advice — not as a substitute for emergency veterinary care when a pet's life may be at risk.
