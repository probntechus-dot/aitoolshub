The One Factor That Should Decide This

It comes down to complexity. If your automation has:

Ease of Use: Zapier Wins Clearly

Zapier's interface is drag-and-drop intuitive. I built my first Zap in 20 minutes. When I onboarded my team, they were creating their own basic Zaps within an afternoon.

Power & Flexibility: Make Wins

Make's scenario builder handles things Zapier simply can't: complex data transformation, loops and iterators, aggregate data across multiple records, error handling branches, and conditional routing that would take 10+ Zaps in Zapier.

AI Integration: Tied (Both Are Good)

Both platforms have excellent native ChatGPT and Claude integration. Building AI steps into your automations works cleanly on both. I'd call this a tie. My Zapier AI guide shows how to implement AI steps in Zapier, and the same principles apply in Make.

My Final Verdict

Choose Zapier if: You're new to automation, you need to onboard a non-technical team, or your workflows are straightforward.

Choose Make if: You have complex multi-step workflows, high task volume (cost sensitivity), or you're comfortable with a learning curve for more power.

Do what I do: Use both. Zapier for simple, frequently-triggered automations. Make for the complex stuff. They're not mutually exclusive and both have free tiers to start.
