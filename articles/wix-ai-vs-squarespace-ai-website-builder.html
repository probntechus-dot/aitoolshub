Wix AI vs Squarespace AI: Which Website Builder Wins?

⏱ 9 min read
By Sarah Mitchell March 14, 2026
📝 Disclosure: Some links in this article are affiliate links. If you purchase through them, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. We only recommend tools we've personally tested.

Last month, I set myself a challenge: build two websites from scratch in a single afternoon. One on Wix, one on Squarespace. Both using their AI tools. Same brief — a portfolio site for a fictional photographer named "Ava Chen."

The goal was simple: see which platform's AI actually helps, and which one just adds buzzwords to its marketing page.

Two hours later, I had two functioning websites and some strong opinions. Here's everything I learned.

The AI Site Builders: How They Work

Wix AI Site Generator

Wix goes all in on AI-generated websites. When you start a new site, you're greeted by what feels like a conversation. The AI asks you questions: What's your business type? What features do you need? What's your style — bold, minimal, classic? Do you want a blog? Online booking? A store?

After about 90 seconds of questions, it generates a complete website. I'm talking homepage, about page, contact page, portfolio gallery — all populated with placeholder text and stock images that actually match the photography niche. The layout was modern, the color scheme made sense, and the typography was clean.

Was it perfect? No. The generated text was generic ("Welcome to Ava Chen Photography, where we capture your most precious moments"). But the structure was solid. I had a working site skeleton in under two minutes.

Squarespace Blueprint

Squarespace takes a more measured approach. Instead of generating an entire site at once, Blueprint walks you through design decisions step by step. You pick a layout style, choose your pages, select fonts, and set colors. AI assists at each step with recommendations based on your industry and preferences.

The result was more polished out of the box — Squarespace's design sensibility is just better. The spacing, the typography pairing, the way images interact with text — it all felt more intentional. But it took about 15 minutes instead of two, because you're making decisions at each stage rather than letting AI handle everything.

Squarespace also has a separate AI text tool that can generate and refine copy across your site. It's good at adjusting tone — "make this more professional" or "shorten this" — but it doesn't auto-populate your entire site the way Wix does.

Templates and Design Quality

Let's talk about what these sites actually look like.

Squarespace has been the design benchmark for website builders since the early 2010s, and that reputation is still deserved. Every template looks like it was designed by someone who actually knows what they're doing. The layouts are structured, the whitespace is generous, and the overall aesthetic is premium without being pretentious.

With Squarespace, it's hard to build an ugly site. The trade-off? You have less freedom to move things around. The templates are opinionated — they look great within their constraints, but if you want to put a button here instead of there, you might not be able to.

Wix offers over 900 templates, significantly more than Squarespace's 150+. The quality is more variable — some Wix templates look fantastic, others feel dated. But Wix gives you complete drag-and-drop freedom. You can put anything anywhere. Move that heading two pixels to the left? Done. Stack three columns in a weird asymmetrical layout? Go for it.

That freedom is a double-edged sword. I've seen beautiful Wix sites, and I've seen Wix sites that look like a ransom note. Squarespace's guardrails prevent disasters; Wix's freedom allows both masterpieces and messes.

Ease of Use

Squarespace has a gentler learning curve for non-designers. Because the templates constrain what you can do, there are fewer decisions to make and fewer ways to break things. Most people can build a presentable Squarespace site in an afternoon without watching a single tutorial.

Wix has a steeper initial learning curve — the editor has more buttons, more panels, more options. But once you understand the drag-and-drop system (which takes maybe an hour), you can build virtually anything without touching code. The AI features also flatten the learning curve significantly. Instead of figuring out where to put things, you let the AI make a first pass and then adjust.

For absolute beginners who just want something that looks good: Squarespace. For people who want control and are willing to spend an extra hour learning: Wix.

SEO Tools

Both platforms handle SEO fundamentals competently. You can customize page titles, meta descriptions, URL slugs, image alt text, and header tags. Both generate XML sitemaps, both are mobile-responsive, and both support SSL certificates.

Wix has a built-in SEO Wiz (rebranded as "SEO Setup Checklist") that walks you through optimization step by step. It tells you exactly what to do — add a meta description here, set up Google Search Console there. For beginners who don't know what SEO is, this guided approach is incredibly helpful.

Squarespace doesn't hold your hand the same way, but its default code is cleaner. Pages tend to load faster out of the box, semantic HTML is better structured, and the platform handles technical SEO elements (canonical tags, structured data for products) more elegantly.

For non-technical users who need guidance: Wix's SEO Wiz is more helpful. For technical SEO performance: Squarespace has a slight edge in page speed and code quality.

Squarespace's commerce features feel more polished and integrated. Product pages look better by default, the checkout flow is smoother, and inventory management is cleaner. If you're selling physical products, digital downloads, or services, Squarespace's built-in tools are excellent.

Wix compensates with its app marketplace. Need a specific shipping calculator? A loyalty program? Advanced product filtering? There's probably a Wix app for it. Squarespace's extension library is much smaller, so if the built-in features don't cover your needs, you have fewer options.

Wix's free plan is worth mentioning. You can build and publish a site for free, though it'll show Wix branding and ads. Squarespace only offers a 14-day trial. If you're testing the waters or building a personal project on a budget, Wix's free tier is a genuine advantage.

Who Should Pick What

Choose Wix AI if:

  • You want a complete AI-generated site in minutes as your starting point
  • You need total design freedom and drag-and-drop flexibility
  • You want access to hundreds of third-party apps and integrations
  • You're on a tight budget and want to start free
  • You need specific functionality that requires an app or plugin

Choose Squarespace AI if:

  • Design quality is your top priority and you want a site that looks professional with minimal effort
  • You prefer structured templates that prevent design mistakes
  • You're building an ecommerce store and want polished, built-in commerce tools
  • You value clean code and fast page speeds
  • You want AI-powered text generation integrated into the editor

🏆 The Verdict

Wix AI is the better choice if you want speed and flexibility. Its AI site generator is genuinely impressive for getting a working site fast, and the drag-and-drop editor gives you unlimited creative control. Squarespace AI is the better choice if you prioritize aesthetics and polish. Its templates are consistently beautiful, its commerce features are more refined, and the AI text tools integrate smoothly into the editing experience. For my fictional photographer Ava? I'd pick Squarespace. For a local business that needs a site up today? Wix, without question.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Wix AI really build a website for me?

Yes. Wix's AI Site Generator creates a complete multi-page website in under two minutes based on your answers to a few questions. You'll want to customize the content and images, but the structure and layout are genuinely usable right out of the gate.

Does Squarespace have AI features?

Yes. Squarespace Blueprint uses AI to help design your site layout, and Squarespace AI generates and refines text content across your pages. The AI is more subtle than Wix's all-at-once approach but integrates smoothly into the editor.

Which builder is better for ecommerce?

For polished, built-in ecommerce with great product pages and smooth checkout: Squarespace. For more flexibility and third-party app integrations: Wix. Both handle small-to-medium stores well.

Is Wix or Squarespace better for SEO?

Both handle SEO fundamentals well. Wix has a guided SEO Wiz that's great for beginners. Squarespace has cleaner default code and faster page speeds, giving it a slight edge in technical SEO performance.

Can I switch from Wix to Squarespace (or vice versa)?

There's no automatic migration tool. You'd need to rebuild your pages manually. Blog posts can be exported, but designs, pages, and store data must be recreated. Choose carefully to avoid the hassle of switching later.
