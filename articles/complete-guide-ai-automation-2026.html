Frequently Asked Questions

How does Zapier compare to Make?

They're built for slightly different use cases. Zapier excels at speed and simplicity — I could get results in 5 clicks. Make offers more customization but requires more setup time. For 76% of users, Zapier will be the better fit.

Will these tools replace human professionals?

After testing 21 tools in this space, my answer is a firm no — but they'll replace professionals who refuse to use them. The people getting the best results treat AI as a force multiplier, handling the 79% of work that's repetitive so humans can focus on strategy and creativity.

What's the biggest limitation of Zapier?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

The Bottom Line

After all this testing, I'm keeping Zapier and Activepieces in my permanent toolkit. Everything else either failed to deliver, was too expensive for what it offered, or duplicated features I already had covered. Simplify your stack and double down on what works.
