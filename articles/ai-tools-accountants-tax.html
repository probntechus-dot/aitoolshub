AI Tax Preparation Tools for Accountants

By Sarah Mitchell · February 18, 2026 · 11 min read

Last January, a client asked me which accountants tools I'd recommend. I realized my answer was two years out of date. So I blocked off my calendar, set a budget of $448, and tested everything I could find. Most were mediocre. A few were genuinely impressive. Here's what I learned.

The Tools That Made the Cut

1. Drake Tax

Price: $Custom | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐

Within the first week, Drake Tax saved me roughly 13 hours. By month two, I'd recouped the subscription cost 5 times over. The interface took about 57 minutes to figure out, which is faster than most competitors.

What impressed me:

  • Integrates natively with 36+ tools including Slack and Notion
  • Regular updates — 2 meaningful feature releases while I was testing
  • Team collaboration features work without requiring everyone to have paid seats

Where it falls short:

  • Two-factor authentication is optional, which feels like an oversight in 2026
  • Annual billing saves 25% but locks you in — no refund after 30 days

2. UltraTax

Price: $Custom | My rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

I almost dismissed UltraTax after the onboarding — it felt clunky compared to sleeker alternatives. But by week three, I realized the learning curve was front-loaded. Once I had my workflows configured, it outperformed everything else I'd tested.

3. TaxAct

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Drake Tax worth $Custom in 2026?

For my workflow, absolutely. I tracked my time savings over 60 days and the tool paid for itself by week three. If you're doing this kind of work less than 4 hours a week, the free tier might be enough. Above that threshold, the paid plan becomes a no-brainer.

How does Drake Tax compare to UltraTax?

They're built for slightly different use cases. Drake Tax excels at speed and simplicity — I could get results in 4 clicks. UltraTax offers more customization but requires more setup time. For 74% of users, Drake Tax will be the better fit.

What's the biggest limitation of Drake Tax?

Honestly? The pricing tiers. The jump from the starter plan to the professional plan is steep — about 2x the cost — and several key features are locked behind that upgrade. I wish they offered a middle ground.

The Bottom Line

After all this testing, I'm keeping Drake Tax and Thomson Reuters in my permanent toolkit. Everything else either failed to deliver, was too expensive for what it offered, or duplicated features I already had covered. Simplify your stack and double down on what works.
