Jasper vs Copy.ai vs Writesonic: I Spent $400 Testing These AI Writers So You Don't Have To

AL
Amanda Liu
Content Marketing Director
üìÖ February 2024 ‚è± 15 min read

Here's a confession: I run content marketing for a B2B company, and my boss gave me a budget to "figure out this AI writing thing." So I did. Aggressively.

I subscribed to Jasper, Copy.ai, and Writesonic simultaneously for one month each. Same tasks, same topics, same standards. I even had my team blind-test outputs without knowing which tool created them.

The results surprised me. Not because any tool was perfect, but because they're all good at completely different things. Picking the wrong one for your use case is an expensive mistake.

üéØ Quick Verdict (Read This If You're in a Hurry)

  • Jasper: Best for marketing teams with an established brand voice
  • Copy.ai: Best for solo creators and short-form content at scale
  • Writesonic: Best for budget-conscious users who need SEO-focused content

The Testing Methodology

I gave each tool the same 12 tasks over 30 days:

  • 4 long-form blog posts (1,200+ words each)
  • 8 social media posts per platform (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram)
  • 3 email sequences (5 emails each)
  • 5 product descriptions
  • 2 case studies
  • 10 ad headlines and copy variants

Three team members blind-rated outputs on quality, brand fit, and how much editing was needed. I tracked how long each task took. The results were logged in a massive spreadsheet I'll never look at again but am weirdly proud of.

Jasper is expensive. There, I said it. When I first looked at the pricing, I almost didn't bother testing it. I'm glad I did.

The Brand Voice feature is genuinely impressive. I uploaded 20 pieces of our existing content, our style guide, and our tone description. Within a day, Jasper was producing content that my team rated as "sounds like us" 78% of the time without any editing. That's remarkable.

Long-form blog posts were Jasper's strongest area. It maintains coherent arguments across 1,500 words, doesn't repeat itself, and usually has a logical structure. The SEO mode pulls in real search data and suggests optimizations that actually improved our rankings.

Copy.ai

Copy.ai surprised me. I expected it to be a budget alternative to Jasper. It's actually optimized for a completely different workflow.

Where Jasper excels at depth, Copy.ai excels at speed. Their "Workflows" feature lets you set up multi-step content processes that run automatically. I built a workflow that takes a product URL, scrapes the key features, and generates 10 different social posts in different tones. Takes 90 seconds from URL to 10 finished posts.

For our ad creative testing, Copy.ai was my favorite. I could generate 30 headline variants for an A/B test in minutes. Quality was maybe 80% of Jasper, but speed was 3x and cost was a fraction.

Writesonic is the cheapest of the three and honestly holds its own better than I expected. Their Article Writer 5.0 is legitimately good ‚Äî it researches topics, cites sources, and produces SEO-ready articles that don't require much editing.

What I liked most was the Surfer SEO integration. Writesonic analyzes top-ranking content for your target keyword and adjusts your article to match what Google seems to favor. Three articles I wrote with this integration ranked in the top 10 within 6 weeks. Hard to argue with results.

The personality, though. Writesonic tends to write in a fairly similar voice regardless of your brand guidelines. Everything sounds a bit... corporate? Not bad corporate, but noticeably less distinct than what Jasper produces.

My Honest Recommendation by User Type

If you're a solo blogger or content creator: Start with Copy.ai's free plan. If you need more volume, their $49/month Pro is genuinely worth it. Jasper at $49/month is harder to justify when you're alone.

If you're a marketing team of 2-5 people: Jasper Teams. The brand voice consistency alone will save you editing hours. The team collaboration features mean everyone produces content that sounds like one voice.

If you're primarily focused on SEO and blog traffic: Writesonic plus Surfer SEO. The combination is powerful and cheaper than the alternatives.

If budget is tight: Honestly? ChatGPT Plus at $20/month can replace all three of these with the right prompts. It requires more skill but the flexibility is unmatched.

üí° The Real Truth About AI Writing Tools

None of these tools write for you ‚Äî they write with you. The quality of your output is directly proportional to the quality of your brief. A mediocre brief produces mediocre content from any of these tools. Learn to brief AI well and all three become dramatically better.
